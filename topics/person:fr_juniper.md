Brother Juniper (died 1258) was one of the original companions of Francis, famous for his childlike simplicity and literal love of the poor. Francis is said to have remarked, ‘Would that I had a whole forest of such junipers.’ Many tender and comic anecdotes about him survive in Franciscan tradition.
