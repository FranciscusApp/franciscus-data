Saint Clare of Assisi (1194–1253) was the first female follower of Saint Francis and founder of the Order of Poor Ladies, later known as the Poor Clares. Drawn by Francis's preaching, she left the noble Offreduccio family in 1212 to embrace a life of radical poverty at San Damiano. She fiercely defended the "Privilegium paupertatis" and was the first woman to write a monastic rule for her own sisters.
