Al-Kamil (c. 1177–1238) was the Ayyubid sultan of Egypt and nephew of Saladin. During the Fifth Crusade, in 1219, he received Francis peacefully at Damietta — a famous encounter remembered as a model of dialogue between Christians and Muslims.
