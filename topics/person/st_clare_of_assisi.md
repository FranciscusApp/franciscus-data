Saint Clare of Assisi (1194–1253) was the first female follower of Saint Francis and the founder of the Order of the Poor Clares. Inspired by Francis’s preaching, she left the noble Offreduccio family in 1212 to embrace a life of radical poverty at San Damiano. Clare wanted to live according to the rule of strict poverty preached by Francis, a practice that at the time was considered incompatible with the strict enclosure imposed on nuns. For years, she passionately sought approval for her spiritual vision, until she became the first woman to write a monastic rule that was officially approved for her own sisters.
