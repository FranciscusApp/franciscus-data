# The Office of the Passion

## Introduction <a id="intro"></a>

<p id="offpass-1">
Here begin the psalms which our most blessed father Francis arranged in reverence and memory and praise of the Passion of the Lord. These are to be recited, one at each of the hours of the day and night. And they begin at Compline of Good Friday, because it was on that night that our Lord Jesus Christ was betrayed and seized. And note that blessed Francis recited this office in the following manner: First he said the prayer which the Lord and Master taught us: Most holy Our Father, and so forth, together with the praises, namely: Holy, holy, holy, as is set out above. When the praises with their prayer were finished, he began this antiphon, namely: Holy Mary. First he recited the psalms of holy Mary; afterward he said the other psalms which he had chosen, and at the end of all the psalms that he recited, he said the psalms of the Passion. When the psalm was finished, he said this antiphon, namely: Holy Mary Virgin. When the antiphon was finished, the office was complete.
</p>

Note also that the whole psalm is not recited, but only up to the verse, namely: "Let the whole earth be moved at his presence"; take care, however, to understand that the entire verse is to be said: "Take up your bodies."
