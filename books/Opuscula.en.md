# COMPLETE MINOR WORKS OF SAINT FRANCIS OF ASSISI

## Admonitions <a id="adm"></a>

<aside>
[Chapter I: On the Body of the Lord]
</aside>

<p id="adm-1">
[1] The Lord Jesus says to his disciples: I am the way, the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father except through me. [2] If you had known me, you would surely have known my Father also; and from now on you shall know him and have seen him. [3] Philip says to him: Lord, show us the Father, and it is enough for us. [4] Jesus says to him: Have I been so long a time with you, and you have not known me? Philip, he who sees me <ref to="John 14:6-9">sees my Father</ref> also. [5] <ref to="1 Tim 6:16">The Father dwells in light inaccessible</ref>, <ref to="John 4:24">and God is a spirit</ref>, <ref to="John 1:18">and no one has ever seen God</ref>. [6] Therefore he can be seen only in the spirit, for it is the spirit that gives life; <ref to="John 6:64">the flesh profits nothing</ref>. [7] Yet neither is the Son, in that which he is equal to the Father, seen by anyone otherwise than the Father, otherwise than the Holy Spirit. [8] Hence all who saw the Lord Jesus according to his humanity, and did not see and believe according to the spirit and the divinity that he is the true Son of God, are condemned. [9] So too now, all who see the sacrament that is consecrated by the words of the Lord upon the altar through the hand of the priest in the form of bread and wine, and do not see and believe according to the spirit and the divinity that it is truly the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, are condemned, [10] for the Most High himself bears witness, who says: <ref to="Mark 14:22">This is my body and the blood of my new testament [which shall be shed for many]</ref>; [11] and: He who eats my flesh and drinks my blood <ref to="John 6:55">has eternal life</ref>. [12] Hence it is the Spirit of the Lord, who dwells in his faithful, that receives the most holy body and blood of the Lord. [13] All others, who do not share in that same Spirit and yet presume to receive him, <ref to="1 Cor 11:29">eat and drink judgment upon themselves</ref>.
[14] Therefore: O sons of men, <ref to="Ps 4:3">how long will you be heavy of heart?</ref>. [15] <ref to="John 9:35">Why do you not know the truth and believe in the Son of God?</ref>. [16] Behold, <ref to="Phil 2:8">daily he humbles himself</ref>, <ref to="Wis 18:15">as when from his royal throne</ref> he came into the womb of the Virgin; [17] daily he comes to us, appearing in humility; [18] <ref to="John 1:18">daily he descends from the bosom of the Father</ref> upon the altar into the hands of the priest. [19] And just as he showed himself to the holy apostles in true flesh, so now he shows himself to us in the sacred bread. [20] And just as they, by the gaze of the flesh, saw only his flesh, yet believed him to be God, contemplating him with spiritual eyes, [21] so too let us, seeing bread and wine with our bodily eyes, see and firmly believe that it is his most holy body and blood, living and true. [22] And in this way the Lord is always with his faithful, as he himself says: <ref to="Matt 28:20">Behold, I am with you until the consummation of the age</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter II: On the evil of one's own will]
</aside>

<p id="adm-2">
[1] The Lord said to Adam: Eat of every tree, <ref to="Gen 2:16">but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat</ref>. [2] Of every tree of paradise he was permitted to eat, for so long as he did not act against obedience, he did not sin. [3] For that man eats of the tree of the knowledge of good who claims his own will as his own and exalts himself over the good things that the Lord says and works within him; [4] and thus, through the suggestion of the devil and the transgression of the commandment, it became the fruit of the knowledge of evil. [5] Whence it is fitting that he should bear the punishment.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter III: On Perfect Obedience]
</aside>

<p id="adm-3">
[1] The Lord says in the Gospel: Whoever does not renounce all that he possesses <ref to="Luke 14:33">cannot be my disciple</ref>; [2] and: <ref to="Luke 9:24">Whoever would save his life shall lose it</ref>. [3] That man forsakes all that he possesses and loses his own body who yields himself wholly to obedience into the hands of his superior. [4] And whatever he does and says, provided he knows that it is not against his superior's will and that what he does is good, this is true obedience. [5] And if at any time a subject should see things better and more useful for his own soul than those which his superior commands him, let him willingly sacrifice his own will to God; but as for what belongs to his superior, let him strive to fulfill it in deed. [6] <ref to="1 Pet 1:22">For this is charitable obedience</ref>, since it gives satisfaction to God and to neighbor. [7] But if a superior should command anything against his soul, though he need not obey him, still let him not abandon him. [8] And if on that account he should endure persecution from certain persons, let him love them all the more for God's sake. [9] For he who endures persecution rather than wish to be separated from his brothers truly abides in perfect obedience, <ref to="John 15:13">because he lays down his life</ref> for his brothers. [10] For there are many religious who, under the pretense of seeing something better than what their superiors command, <ref to="Luke 9:62">look backward</ref><ref to="Prov 26:11; 2 Pet 2:22">and return to the vomit of their own will</ref>; [11] these are murderers, and by their evil example they cause many souls to be lost.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter IV: That no one should claim prelacy for himself]
</aside>

<p id="adm-4">
[1] "I came not to be served, <ref to="Matt 20:28">but to serve </ref>," says the Lord. [2] Let those who are set over others glory in that office of authority only so far as they would if they were assigned the task of washing the feet of the brothers. [3] And the more they are troubled at having their office of authority taken from them than at losing the task of washing feet, <ref to="John 12:6">the more they lay up purses to the peril of their souls </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter V: That no one should be proud, but should glory in the cross of the Lord]
</aside>

<p id="adm-5">
[1] Consider, O man, in what surpassing dignity the Lord God has established you, <ref to="Gen 1:26">for he created and formed you after the image of his beloved Son in the body and after his likeness</ref> in the spirit. [2] And all creatures that are beneath the heavens, each in its own way, serve, acknowledge, and obey their Creator better than you do. [3] And even the demons did not crucify him, but you, together with them, crucified him, and you crucify him still by taking delight in vices and sins. [4] <ref to="1 Cor 13:2">Wherefore, then, can you boast? [5] For even if you were so keen and wise that you possessed all knowledge</ref><ref to="1 Cor 12:28"> and knew how to interpret every kind of tongue</ref> and to search out with subtlety the things of heaven, in none of these could you glory; [6] for a single demon knew of heavenly things, and now knows of earthly things, more than all men, however great a man there may have been who received from the Lord a special knowledge of the highest wisdom. [7] Likewise, even if you were fairer and richer than all others, and even if you worked wonders so as to put demons to flight, all these things are against you and belong to you in no way, and in them you can take no glory; [8] <ref to="2 Cor 12:5">but in this we can glory, in our infirmities</ref><ref to="Luke 14:27"> and in bearing daily the holy cross of our Lord Jesus Christ</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VI: On the Imitation of the Lord]
</aside>

<p id="adm-6">
[1] Let us all take heed, brothers, of the Good Shepherd, who to save his sheep endured the passion of the cross. [2] The sheep of the Lord followed him through tribulation and persecution, through shame and hunger, through infirmity and temptation and all the rest; and for these things they received from the Lord life everlasting. [3] Wherefore it is a great shame for us, the servants of God, that the saints performed the works, while we, merely by recounting them, wish to receive glory and honor.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VII: That good works should follow upon knowledge]
</aside>

<p id="adm-7">
[1] The Apostle says: The letter kills, <ref to="2 Cor 3:6">but the spirit gives life</ref>. [2] Those are slain by the letter who desire to know words alone, that they may be held wiser than others and may be able to acquire great riches to bestow upon their kinsmen and friends. [3] And those religious are slain by the letter who refuse to follow the spirit of the divine letter, but desire only to know the words the more and to expound them to others. [4] And those are quickened by the spirit of the divine letter who do not claim for themselves any letter that they know or desire to know, but by word and example render it back to the most high Lord God, to whom belongs every good.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VIII: On avoiding the sin of envy]
</aside>

<p id="adm-8">
[1] The Apostle says: No one can say, 'Lord Jesus,' <ref to="1 Cor 12:3">except in the Holy Spirit</ref>; [2] and: There is none who does good, <ref to="Rom 3:12">no, not so much as one</ref>. [3] Whoever, therefore, envies his brother the good that the Lord speaks and works within him falls into the sin of blasphemy, <ref to="Matt 20:15">for he envies the Most High himself</ref>, who speaks and works every good.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter IX: On Love]
</aside>

<p id="adm-9">
[1] The Lord says: Love your enemies, [do good to those who hate you, <ref to="Matt 5:44">and pray for those who persecute and slander you]</ref>. [2] For that man truly loves his enemy who grieves not over the wrong done to himself, [3] but is set ablaze on account of the love of God over the sin committed against his enemy's own soul. [4] And let him show his love toward that man through his deeds.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter X: On the Chastisement of the Body]
</aside>

<p id="adm-10">
[1] There are many who, when they sin or suffer some wrong, are quick to lay the blame upon an enemy or a neighbor. [2] But it is not so; for each one holds his enemy within his own power — namely, the body, through which he sins. [3] <ref to="Matt 24:46">Therefore blessed is that servant</ref> who keeps such an enemy, delivered into his own power, ever held captive, and wisely guards himself against it; [4] for so long as he does this, no other enemy, visible or invisible, will be able to harm him.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XI: That no one be corrupted by another's evil]
</aside>

<p id="adm-11">
[1] Nothing ought to displease the servant of God save sin alone. [2] And in whatever manner any person might sin, if on this account the servant of God is disturbed and grows angry not out of charity, <ref to="Rom 2:5">he stores up guilt for himself</ref>. [3] That servant of God who is not angered and does not disquiet himself over anyone lives rightly without anything of his own. [4] And blessed is he who keeps back nothing for himself, rendering to Caesar the things that are Caesar's, <ref to="Matt 22:21">and to God the things that are God's</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XII: On Discerning the Spirit of the Lord]
</aside>

<p id="adm-12">
[1] Thus may the servant of God be recognized, whether he possesses something of the Spirit of the Lord: [2] when the Lord works some good through him, if his flesh does not on that account exalt itself, since the flesh is ever opposed to every good, [3] but if he rather holds himself the more contemptible in his own eyes and reckons himself lesser than all other men.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XIII: On Patience]
</aside>

<p id="adm-13">
[1] Blessed are the peacemakers, <ref to="Matt 5:9">for they shall be called the children of God</ref>. A servant of God cannot know how much patience and humility he possesses within himself so long as he is given his due. [2] But when the time comes in which those who ought to satisfy him do the opposite, then whatever measure of patience and humility he shows in that moment is precisely the measure he has — and no more.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XIV: On poverty of spirit]
</aside>

<p id="adm-14">
[1] Blessed are the poor in spirit, <ref to="Matt 5:3">for theirs is the kingdom of heaven</ref>. [2] There are many who, devoting themselves to prayers and offices, impose upon their bodies many abstinences and mortifications, [3] yet at a single word that seems to slight their bodies, or over some thing that might be taken from them, they are at once scandalized and thrown into turmoil. [4] These are not poor in spirit; for whoever is truly poor in spirit <ref to="Matt 5:39">hates himself and loves those who strike him on the cheek</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XV: On Peace]
</aside>

<p id="adm-15">
[1] Blessed are the peacemakers, <ref to="Matt 5:9">for they shall be called the sons of God</ref>. [2] Those are the true peacemakers who, amid all that they suffer in this world, keep peace in soul and body for the love of our Lord Jesus Christ.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVI: On Purity of Heart]
</aside>

<p id="adm-16">
[1] Blessed are the pure in heart, <ref to="Matt 5:8">for they shall see God</ref>. [2] Truly pure in heart are those who scorn the things of earth and seek the things of heaven, who with a clean heart and mind never cease to adore and behold the Lord God, the living and the true.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVII: On the humble servant of God]
</aside>

<p id="adm-17">
[1] <ref to="Matt 24:46"> Blessed is that servant </ref> who exalts himself no more over the good that the Lord says and works through him than over what He says and works through another. [2] A man sins who would rather receive from his neighbor than give of himself to the Lord God.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVIII: On Compassion for One's Neighbor]
</aside>

<p id="adm-18">
[1] Blessed is the man who bears with his neighbor in his frailty as he himself would wish to be borne with, <ref to="Gal 6:2; Matt 7:12">were he in a like case</ref>. [2] Blessed is the servant who renders back to the Lord God every good thing, <ref to="Matt 25:18">for whoever holds back anything for himself conceals within himself the money of his Lord God</ref>, and what he thought he possessed <ref to="Luke 8:18">shall be taken away from him</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XIX: On the humble servant of God]
</aside>

<p id="adm-19">
[1] Blessed is the servant who does not think himself the better when he is magnified and exalted by men, but holds himself just as he would when reckoned worthless, lowly, and despised; [2] for what a man is before God, that he is and nothing more. [3] Woe to that religious who is set on high by others and by his own will refuses to come down. [4] <ref to="Matt 24:46">And blessed is that servant</ref> who is not set on high by his own will and ever longs to be beneath the feet of others.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XX: On the good religious and the vain religious]
</aside>

<p id="adm-20">
[1] Blessed is that religious <ref to="Ps 50:10">who finds no delight and gladness save in the most holy words and works of the Lord, [2] and who by these leads people to the love of God with joy and gladness</ref>. [3] Woe to that religious who delights in idle and empty words, and by these leads people to laughter.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXI: On the vain and talkative religious]
</aside>

<p id="adm-21">
[1] Blessed is the servant who, when he speaks, <ref to="Prov 29:20">does not reveal all he has for the sake of reward, and is not hasty in speaking </ref>, but wisely considers what he ought to say and answer. [2] Woe to that religious who <ref to="Luke 2:19">does not keep in his heart </ref> the good things the Lord has shown him, and does not reveal them to others through his deeds, but rather, for the sake of reward, longs to display them to men in words. [3] <ref to="Matt 6:2">He receives his reward </ref>, and those who hear carry away little fruit.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXII: On Correction]
</aside>

<p id="adm-22">
[1] Blessed is the servant who would bear discipline, accusation, and rebuke from another as patiently as he would from himself. [2] Blessed is the servant who, when rebuked, graciously submits, modestly obeys, humbly confesses, and willingly makes amends. [3] Blessed is the servant who is not swift to excuse himself, and who humbly endures the shame and rebuke for a sin, even when he has committed no fault.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXIII: On Humility]
</aside>

<p id="adm-23">
[1] Blessed is the servant who is found as humble among his subjects as he would be among his masters. [2] Blessed is the servant who remains ever beneath the rod of correction. [3] <ref to="Matt 24:45"> He is a faithful and prudent servant </ref> who, in all his offenses, does not delay to punish himself inwardly through contrition and outwardly through confession and the satisfaction of works.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXIV: On True Love]
</aside>

<p id="adm-24">
Blessed is the servant who would love his brother as much when he is sick and cannot repay him, as when he is well and can repay him.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXV: Again, concerning the same]
</aside>

<p id="adm-25">
Blessed is that servant who would love and revere his brother when he is far from him just as he does when he is with him, and who would say nothing behind his back that he could not, in charity, say to his face.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXVI: That the servants of God should honor clerics]
</aside>

<p id="adm-26">
[1] Blessed is the servant who keeps faith in those clergy who live rightly according to the pattern of the Roman Church. [2] And woe to those who despise them; for although they be sinners, still no one ought to judge them, since the Lord alone reserves them to himself for judgment. [3] For the greater the ministry entrusted to them concerning the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, which they themselves receive and they alone administer to others, [4] so much the graver is the sin of those who sin against them, more than against all other men of this world.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXVII: On the Virtue that Drives Out Vice]
</aside>

<p id="adm-27">
[1] Where there is charity and wisdom, there is neither fear nor ignorance.
[2] Where there is patience and humility, there is neither anger nor disquiet.
[3] Where there is poverty with gladness, there is neither greed nor avarice.
[4] Where there is repose and meditation, there is neither anxiety nor restless wandering.
[5] <ref to="Luke 11:21"> Where there is fear of the Lord to guard His courtyard </ref>, there the enemy can find no place to enter.
[6] Where there is mercy and discernment, there is neither excess nor hardness of heart.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXVIII: On concealing what is good, lest it be lost]
</aside>

<p id="adm-28">
[1] Blessed is the servant who <ref to="Matt 6:20">stores up in heaven </ref> the good things which the Lord reveals to him, and who, in hope of reward, does not long to make them known to men, [2] for the Most High himself will make his works manifest to whomever He pleases. [3] Blessed is the servant who <ref to="Luke 2:19">keeps the secrets of the Lord within his heart </ref>.
</p>

## Listen, little poor ones <a id="audite"></a>

<p id="audite-1">
[1] Hear me, little poor ones called by the Lord, you who from many regions and provinces are gathered together: live always in truth, that you may die in obedience. [2] Look not to the outward life, for that of the spirit is the better. I beg you with great love to have discretion in the alms which the Lord gives you. [3] Those who are burdened with infirmity, and the others who wear themselves out in caring for them — bear it all in peace, [4] for you shall sell this labor very dearly, since each of you will be crowned a queen in heaven together with the Virgin Mary.
</p>

## The Blessing Given to Brother Bernard <a id="benbern"></a>

<p id="benbern-1">
[1] Write down what I tell you: [2] The first brother whom the Lord gave me was Brother Bernard, and it was he who first began and most perfectly fulfilled the perfection of the holy Gospel by distributing all his goods to the poor; [3] and on this account, and by reason of many other privileges, I am bound to love him more than any other brother of the whole religious order. [4] Wherefore I will and command, so far as I am able, that whoever shall be minister general love and honor him as he would honor me myself, [5] and that the other provincial ministers and the brothers of the whole order likewise hold him in my stead.
</p>

## The Blessing of Saint Clare <a id="bencl"></a>

<p id="bencl-1">
[1] ...to console her, he wrote to her, sending in his letter [2] his blessing, and moreover he absolved her from every failing, if she had committed any, with respect to his own commands and wishes and the commands and wishes of the Son of God.
</p>

## The blessing given to Brother Leo <a id="benleo"></a>

<p id="benleo-1">
[1] May the Lord bless you and keep you; may he show his face to you and have mercy upon you. [2] <ref to="Num 6:24-26"> May he turn his countenance toward you and grant you peace </ref>. [3] The Lord bless you, Brother Leo, <ref to="Num 6:27">you </ref>.
</p>

## The Canticle of Brother Sun <a id="cantsol"></a>

<p id="cantsol-1">
[1] Most High, all-powerful, good Lord, yours are the praises, the glory, and the honor, and every blessing. [2] To you alone, Most High, do they belong, and no man is worthy to speak your name. [3] Be praised, my Lord, with all your creatures, especially through Sir Brother Sun, who brings the day, and by whom you give us light. [4] And he is beautiful and radiant with great splendor; of you, Most High, he bears the likeness. [5] Be praised, my Lord, through Sister Moon and the stars: in heaven you have formed them clear and precious and fair. [6] Be praised, my Lord, through Brother Wind, and through the air, both cloudy and serene, and every kind of weather, by which you sustain your creatures. [7] Be praised, my Lord, through Sister Water, who is most useful and humble and precious and chaste. [8] Be praised, my Lord, through Brother Fire, by whom you light up the night; and he is beautiful and joyful and mighty and strong. [9] Be praised, my Lord, through our Sister Mother Earth, who sustains and governs us, and brings forth diverse fruits with colored flowers and grass. [10] Be praised, my Lord, through those who grant pardon for love of you, and endure sickness and tribulation. [11] Blessed are those who endure in peace, for by you, Most High, they shall be crowned. [12] Be praised, my Lord, through our Sister Bodily Death, from whom no living man can escape. [13] Woe to those who die in mortal sin; blessed are those whom she will find within your most holy will, for the second death shall do them no harm. [14] Praise and bless my Lord, and give thanks and serve him with great humility.
</p>

## A Letter to Saint Anthony <a id="epant"></a>

<p id="epant-1">
[1] To Brother Anthony, my bishop, Brother Francis sends greeting. [2] It pleases me that you should teach sacred theology to the brothers, provided that amid this study you do not extinguish the spirit of prayer and devotion, as is contained in the Rule.
</p>

## A Letter to the Citizens of Bologna <a id="epbon"></a>

<p id="epbon-1">
He [namely, Brother Martin of Bartona] also related that a certain friar, who was standing in prayer at Brescia on the day of the Lord's Nativity during the earthquake which Saint Francis had foretold and had caused to be proclaimed by the friars throughout all the schools of Bologna, by means of a letter in which there was faulty Latin, was found unharmed beneath the collapse of the stones when the church fell.
</p>

## A Letter of St. Clare concerning Fasting <a id="epcl"></a>

<p id="epcl-1">
[1] Concerning these matters, which you have now bidden me disclose to you—namely, what the feasts were on which our most glorious father Saint Francis specially admonished us to keep a celebration, feasts which, as I suppose, you have somehow imagined lay in a variety of foods—I have judged it fitting to answer your charity: [2] Let your prudence know indeed that, apart from the weak and the sick, for whom he admonished and charged us to make every allowance we could regarding whatever foods, none of us who is healthy and strong should eat anything but Lenten fare only, on ferial days as much as on feast days, fasting every single day except on Sundays and the Nativity of the Lord, on which we were to eat twice in the day. [3] And likewise on Thursdays at the customary seasons it was left to the will of each, so that she who did not wish it was not bound to fast. [4] We who are healthy, however, fast daily except on Sundays and the Nativity. [5] But throughout all of Eastertide, as the writing of blessed Francis says, and on the feasts of holy Mary and of the holy Apostles, we are likewise not bound to fast, unless these feasts should fall on a Friday; [6] and, as has been said before, always, we who are healthy and strong eat Lenten fare.
</p>

## First Letter to the Clergy <a id="epcler1"></a>

<p id="epcler1-1">
[1] Let us all take heed, we who are clerics, of the grave sin and ignorance which some display toward the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and toward his most sacred names and the words written down that sanctify the body. [2] For we know that there can be no body unless it be first sanctified by the word. [3] Indeed we have and behold bodily in this world nothing of the Most High himself save his body and blood, his names and words, <ref to="1 John 3:14">through which we have been made and redeemed from death to life</ref>. [4] Let all those who minister mysteries so holy consider within themselves — above all those who minister unworthily — how mean are the chalices, the corporals, and the linens where the body and blood of that same Lord are made a sacrifice. [5] And in many places it is set down and left in unworthy spots, carried about wretchedly, received unworthily, and dispensed to others without discernment. [6] His names too and his written words are at times trampled underfoot; [7] <ref to="1 Cor 2:14">for the animal man does not perceive the things that are of God</ref>. [8] Are we not moved by devotion at all this, when the merciful Lord himself gives himself into our hands, and we handle him and daily take him into our mouths? [9] Or are we ignorant that we must come into his hands? [10] Therefore let us swiftly and firmly amend all these things and others besides; [11] and wherever the most holy body of our Lord Jesus Christ has been unlawfully placed and abandoned, let it be removed from that place and set and enshrined in a precious spot. [12] Likewise the names and written words of the Lord, wherever they are found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and set in a fitting place. [13] To all these things, unto the end of the world, all clerics are bound to attend above all else. [14] And those who do not do so <ref to="Matt 12:36">let them know that they must render account before our Lord Jesus Christ on the day of judgment</ref>. [15] And that this writing may be the better observed, let those who make copies of it know that they are blessed by the Lord God.
</p>

## Second Letter to the Clergy <a id="epcler2"></a>

<p id="epcler2-1">
[1] Let us take heed, all we clerics, of the grievous sin and the ignorance that certain men display toward the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and toward his most sacred names and written words, which consecrate the body. [2] For we know that there can be no body unless it is first consecrated by the word. [3] Indeed, in this world we possess and behold bodily nothing of the Most High himself except the body and blood, the names and words, <ref to="1 John 3:14">through which we were made and redeemed from death unto life</ref>. [4] Let all those who administer such most holy mysteries consider within themselves—above all those who administer them without discernment—how mean are the chalices, the corporals, and the linens upon which the body and blood of our Lord is sacrificed. [5] And by many it is left in unworthy places, carried about miserably, received unworthily, and dispensed to others without discernment. [6] His written names and words are at times trampled underfoot; [7] <ref to="1 Cor 2:14">for the natural man does not perceive the things that are of God</ref>. [8] Are we not stirred to devotion by all these things, when the merciful Lord himself gives himself into our hands, and we handle him and daily receive him upon our lips? [9] Or are we ignorant that we must come into his hands? [10] Therefore let us swiftly and firmly amend ourselves in all these matters and others besides; [11] and wherever the most holy body of our Lord Jesus Christ may have been unlawfully placed and abandoned, let it be removed from that place and set and secured in a precious place. [12] Likewise the written names and words of the Lord, wherever they are found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and be placed in a fitting spot. [13] And we know that we are bound to observe all these things above all else, according to the commands of the Lord and the constitutions of holy mother Church. [14] And whoever fails to do this, let him know that <ref to="Matt 12:36">he shall render an account before our Lord Jesus Christ on the day of judgment</ref>. [15] And that this writing may be the better observed, let those who make copies of it know that they are blessed by the Lord God.
</p>

## First Letter to the Custodians <a id="epcust1"></a>

<p id="epcust1-1">
[1] To all the custodians of the Friars Minor to whom these letters shall come, Brother Francis, your servant and little one in the Lord God, sends greeting together with new signs of heaven and earth, which in God's sight are great and most excellent, yet by many religious and by other men are reckoned as of the least account.
[2] I beseech you, more than on my own behalf, that when it is fitting and you see it to be expedient, you humbly entreat the clergy that above all things they ought to venerate the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and his holy names and written words, which sanctify the body. [3] They ought to hold as precious the chalices, the corporals, the ornaments of the altar, and all things that pertain to the sacrifice. [4] And if in any place the most holy body of the Lord shall have been set in a very poor manner, let it be placed and enshrined by them in a precious spot according to the command of the Church, and let it be carried with great veneration and administered to others with discretion. [5] The names too and the written words of the Lord, wherever they may be found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and set in an honorable place. [6] And in every sermon that you preach, admonish the people concerning penance, and that no one can be saved <ref to="John 6:54">unless he receive the most holy body and blood of the Lord</ref>. [7] And when it is offered in sacrifice by the priest upon the altar and is borne to any place, let all peoples upon bended knees render praise, glory, and honor to the Lord God, the living and the true. [8] And of his praise proclaim and preach thus to all peoples, that at every hour, and whenever the bells are rung, praises and thanks may ever be rendered by the whole people to the almighty God throughout all the earth.
[9] And to whatever of my brother custodians this writing shall come, and who shall copy it and keep it by them, and who shall have it copied for the brothers who hold the office of preaching and the custody of the brothers, and who shall preach all the things contained in this writing unto the end—let them know that they have the blessing of the Lord God and my own. [10] And let these things be for them by true and holy obedience. Amen.
</p>

## Second Letter to the Faithful <a id="epfid2"></a>

<p id="epfid2-1">
In the name of the Lord, the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit. Amen.
[1] To all Christian religious, clerics and laypeople, men and women, to all who dwell throughout the whole world, brother Francis, their servant and subject, offers homage with reverence, true peace from heaven and sincere charity in the Lord.
[2] Since I am the servant of all, I am bound to serve all and to dispense to them the fragrant words of my Lord. [3] Wherefore, pondering in my mind that, since because of the sickness and weakness of my body I cannot visit each one in person, I have resolved by this present letter and by messengers to convey to you the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, who is the Word of the Father, and the words of the Holy Spirit, <ref to="John 6:64">which are spirit and life</ref>.
[4] This Word of the Father, so worthy, so holy and glorious, the most high Father proclaimed from heaven through his holy angel Gabriel into the womb of the holy and glorious Virgin Mary, from whose womb he received the true flesh of our humanity and frailty. [5] He who, <ref to="2 Cor 8:9">though he was rich</ref> above all things, willed himself to choose poverty in the world together with the most blessed Virgin, his mother. [6] And near his passion he celebrated the Passover with his disciples, and taking bread he gave thanks and blessed and broke it, saying: Take and eat, <ref to="Matt 26:26">this is my body</ref>. [7] And taking the cup he said: This is my blood of the new testament, <ref to="Matt 26:27">which shall be poured out for you and for many unto the remission of sins</ref>. [8] Then he prayed to the Father, saying: Father, if it be possible, <ref to="Matt 26:39">let this cup pass from me</ref>. [9] <ref to="Luke 22:44">And his sweat became as drops of blood running down upon the ground</ref>. [10] Yet he placed his own will within the will of the Father, saying: Father, <ref to="Matt 26:42">thy will be done</ref>; not as I will, <ref to="Matt 26:39">but as thou wilt</ref>. [11] Such was the will of that Father, that his blessed and glorious Son, whom he gave to us and who was born for us, should offer himself through his own blood as a sacrifice and victim upon the altar of the cross; [12] not for himself, <ref to="John 1:3">through whom all things were made</ref>, but for our sins, [13] leaving us an example, <ref to="1 Pet 2:21">that we should follow in his footsteps</ref>. [14] And he wills that all of us be saved through him and that we receive him with a pure heart and a chaste body. [15] But few there are who are willing to receive him and to be saved through him, <ref to="Matt 11:30">although his yoke is sweet and his burden light</ref>.
[16] Those who refuse to taste <ref to="Ps 33:9">how sweet the Lord is</ref><ref to="John 3:19">and love darkness rather than light</ref>, unwilling to fulfill the commandments of God, are accursed; [17] of whom it is said through the prophet: <ref to="Ps 118:21">Cursed are they who turn aside from thy commandments</ref>. [18] But, oh how blessed and happy are those who love God and act as the Lord himself says in the gospel: Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with thy whole heart and with thy whole mind, <ref to="Matt 22:37">and thy neighbor as thyself</ref>.
[19] Let us therefore love God and adore him with a pure heart and a pure mind, for he, seeking this above all things, said: <ref to="John 4:23">True worshippers shall adore the Father in spirit and in truth</ref>. [20] For all who adore him <ref to="John 4:24">must adore him in the spirit of truth</ref>. [21] <ref to="Ps 31:4">And let us render to him praises and prayers day and night</ref>, saying: Our Father, <ref to="Matt 6:9">who art in heaven</ref>, <ref to="Luke 18:1">for we ought always to pray and not to faint</ref>.
[22] We ought indeed to confess all our sins to a priest; and let us receive from him the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ. [23] <ref to="John 6:55">He who does not eat his flesh and does not drink his blood</ref> <ref to="John 3:5">cannot enter into the kingdom of God</ref>. [24] Yet let him eat and drink worthily, for whoever receives unworthily eats and drinks judgment to himself, <ref to="1 Cor 11:29">not discerning the body of the Lord</ref>, that is, not distinguishing it. [25] <ref to="Luke 3:8">Let us moreover bring forth fruits worthy of penance</ref>. [26] <ref to="Matt 22:39">And let us love our neighbors as ourselves</ref>. [27] And if anyone is unwilling to love them as himself, let him at least not do them harm, but rather do them good.
[28] But those who have received the power to judge others, let them exercise judgment with mercy, just as they themselves desire to obtain mercy from the Lord. [29] <ref to="Jas 2:13">For judgment without mercy shall be to those who have not shown mercy</ref>. [30] Let us therefore have charity and humility; and let us give alms, <ref to="Tob 4:11; Tob 12:9">for these wash souls from the filth of sins</ref>. [31] For men lose all the things that they leave behind in this world; yet they carry with them the reward of charity and the alms they have given, for which they will have from the Lord a prize and a worthy recompense.
[32] <ref to="Sir 3:32">We ought also to fast and abstain from vices and sins</ref> and from excess of food and drink, and to be Catholic. [33] We ought also to visit churches frequently and to venerate and reverence clerics, not so much on their own account, if they be sinners, but for the office and the administration of the most holy body and blood of Christ, which they sacrifice upon the altar and receive and administer to others. [34] And let us all firmly know that no one can be saved except through the holy words and the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, which the clerics speak, announce, and minister. [35] And they alone ought to minister these, and no others. [36] But especially the religious, who have renounced the world, are bound to do more and greater things, <ref to="Luke 11:42">yet without omitting these</ref>.
[37] We ought to hold our bodies in hatred with their vices and sins, for the Lord says in the gospel: All evils, <ref to="Matt 15:18-19; Mark 7:23">vices and sins proceed from the heart</ref>. [38] We ought to love our enemies and to do good to those <ref to="Matt 5:44; Luke 6:27">who hold us in hatred</ref>. [39] We ought to observe the precepts and counsels of our Lord Jesus Christ. [40] <ref to="Matt 16:24">We ought also to deny ourselves</ref> and to place our bodies beneath the yoke of servitude and holy obedience, just as each one has promised the Lord. [41] And let no one be bound out of obedience to obey another in that wherein an offense or sin is committed.
[42] <ref to="Luke 22:26">But he to whom obedience has been entrusted, and who is held to be greater, let him be as the lesser</ref> and the servant of the other brothers. [43] And toward each of his brothers let him show and have such mercy as he would wish to be shown to himself, <ref to="Matt 7:12">were he in the like case</ref>. [44] Nor let him grow angry at a brother because of his fault, but with all patience and humility let him kindly admonish and bear with him.
[45] We ought not to be wise and prudent according to the flesh, but rather we ought to be simple, humble, and pure. [46] And let us hold our bodies in reproach and contempt, for through our own fault we are all wretched and rotten, foul, and worms, as the Lord says through the prophet: I am a worm and no man, <ref to="Ps 21:7">the reproach of men and the outcast of the people</ref>. [47] Never ought we to desire to be above others, <ref to="1 Pet 2:13">but rather we ought to be servants and subject to every human creature for God's sake</ref>. [48] And all those men and women, when they have done such things and persevered unto the end, <ref to="Isa 11:2">the Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon them</ref><ref to="John 14:23">and shall make within them his dwelling and abode</ref>. [49] <ref to="Matt 5:45">And they shall be children of the heavenly Father</ref>, whose works they do. [50] And they are spouses, <ref to="Matt 12:50">brothers and mothers of our Lord Jesus Christ</ref>. [51] We are spouses when the faithful soul is joined by the Holy Spirit to Jesus Christ. [52] We are brothers, indeed, when we do the will of his Father, <ref to="Matt 12:50">who is in heaven</ref>; [53] mothers, <ref to="1 Cor 6:20">when we bear him in our heart and body</ref> through love and a pure and sincere conscience; we give him birth through holy action, <ref to="Matt 5:16">which ought to shine before others as an example</ref>.
[54] O how glorious and holy and great it is to have a Father in heaven! [55] O how holy it is to have a comforter, beautiful and wonderful, as a spouse! [56] O how holy and how beloved, well-pleasing, humble, peaceful, sweet and lovable and above all things desirable it is to have such a brother and son, <ref to="John 10:15">who laid down his life for his sheep</ref> and prayed to the Father for us, saying: Holy Father, keep in thy name those <ref to="John 17:11">whom thou hast given me</ref>. [57] Father, all whom thou hast given me in the world <ref to="John 17:6">were thine, and thou gavest them to me</ref>. [58] And the words which thou gavest me I gave to them; and they received them and truly knew that I came forth from thee, and they believed <ref to="John 17:8">that thou didst send me</ref>; <ref to="John 17:9">I pray for them and not for the world</ref>; <ref to="John 17:17">bless and sanctify them</ref>. [59] And for them I sanctify myself, <ref to="John 17:19">that they may be sanctified into</ref><ref to="John 17:11">one, as we</ref> are. [60] And I will, Father, that where I am, there they too may be with me, <ref to="John 17:24">that they may behold my glory</ref><ref to="Matt 20:21">in thy kingdom</ref>.
[61] To him, then, who has endured so much for us, who has bestowed and will bestow in the future so many good things, let every creature that is in heaven, on earth, in the sea and in the depths render praise to God, glory, honor, <ref to="Rev 5:13">and blessing</ref>, [62] for he is our strength and our fortitude, who alone is good, alone most high, alone almighty, wonderful, glorious, and alone holy, worthy of praise and blessed through the infinite ages of ages. Amen.
[63] But all those who are not in penance and do not receive the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, [64] and who work vices and sins, and who walk after evil concupiscence and evil desires, and do not observe what they have promised, [65] and who serve the world bodily through carnal desires, the cares and anxieties of this age and the concerns of this life, [66] deceived by the devil, <ref to="John 8:41">whose children they are and whose works they do</ref>, are blind, for they do not see the true light, our Lord Jesus Christ. [67] They have no spiritual wisdom, because they do not have within themselves the Son of God, who is the true wisdom of the Father; of whom it is said: <ref to="Ps 106:27">Their wisdom was swallowed up</ref>. [68] They see, recognize, know, and do evils; and knowingly they lose their souls. [69] Behold, you blind, deceived by our enemies, namely by the flesh, the world, and the devil, for to the body it is sweet to commit sin and bitter to serve God, since all evils, <ref to="Mark 7:21">vices and sins go forth and proceed from the heart of men</ref>, as the Lord says in the gospel. [70] And you have nothing in this world nor in the world to come. [71] You think you will long possess the vanities of this age, but you are deceived, for a day and an hour will come of which you do not think and which you do not know and are ignorant of.
[72] The body sickens, death draws near, kinsmen and friends come, saying: Set your affairs in order. [73] Behold, his wife and his children and his kinsmen and his friends pretend to weep. [74] And looking about he sees them weeping, and is moved by an evil impulse; thinking within himself he says: Behold, I place my soul and my body and all that is mine into your hands. [75] Truly, this man is accursed who trusts and hands over his soul and body and all that is his into such hands; [76] whence the Lord says through the prophet: Cursed is the man <ref to="Jer 17:5">who trusts in man</ref>. [77] And at once they cause a priest to come; the priest says to him: "Do you wish to receive penance for all your sins?" [78] He answers: "I do." "Do you wish to make satisfaction, as far as you can from your substance, for the things you have done and for what you have defrauded and cheated men of?" [79] He answers: "No." And the priest says: "Why not?" [80] "Because I have disposed of everything into the hands of my kinsmen and friends." [81] And he begins to lose the power of speech, and so that wretched man dies.
[82] But let all know that wherever and in whatever manner a man dies in mortal sin without making satisfaction, when he could have made satisfaction and did not, the devil snatches his soul from his body with such anguish and tribulation as no one can know except him who suffers it. [83] And all the talents and power and knowledge <ref to="Luke 8:18">which he thought he had</ref> <ref to="Mark 4:25">shall be taken from him</ref>. [86] In the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit. Amen. [87] I, brother Francis, your lesser servant, ask and beseech you in the charity <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God</ref>, and with the desire to kiss your feet, that you should receive with humility and charity these words and the other words of our Lord Jesus Christ, and act upon them and observe them. [88] And all those men and women who kindly receive them, understand them, and send them to others as an example, <ref to="Matt 24:13">and if they persevere in them unto the end</ref>, may the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit bless them. ## Letter to a Minister <a id="epmin"></a>

<p id="epmin-1">
[1] To Brother N., minister: <ref to="Num 6:24">May the Lord bless you</ref>. [2] I say to you, as best I can, concerning the state of your soul: those things which hinder you from loving the Lord God, and whoever should be an obstacle to you, whether brothers or others, even if they were to strike you, all these things you must count as grace. [3] And thus you must will it, and nothing else. [4] And let this be to you a matter of true obedience to the Lord God and to me, for I know with certainty that this is true obedience. [5] And love those who do these things to you. [6] And do not desire anything else of them save what the Lord shall grant you. [7] And in this love them; and do not wish that they be better Christians. [8] And let this be to you more than any hermitage. [9] And by this I wish to know whether you love the Lord and me his servant and yours: if you do this, namely, that there be no brother in the world who has sinned, however greatly he may have sinned, who, after he has looked into your eyes, ever departs without your mercy, if he seeks mercy. [10] And if he should not seek mercy, do you ask of him whether he wills mercy. [11] And if a thousand times thereafter he should sin before your eyes, love him more than me for this purpose, that you may draw him to the Lord; and always have mercy on such as these. [12] And announce this to the guardians, when you are able, that of your own accord you are thus firmly resolved to do it.
[13] Now concerning all the chapters that are in the rule which speak of mortal sins, with the Lord's help, in the chapter of Pentecost, with the counsel of the brothers, we shall frame this chapter as follows: [14] If any of the brothers, at the prompting of the enemy, should sin mortally, let him be bound by obedience to have recourse to his guardian. [15] And let all the brothers who might know of his sin cause him neither shame nor slander, but let them have great mercy toward him and keep the sin of their brother very private; for they that are whole have no need of a physician, <ref to="Matt 9:12">but they that are sick</ref>. [16] Likewise let them be bound by obedience to send him to his custodian with a companion. [17] And let that custodian mercifully provide for him, as he himself would wish to be provided for <ref to="Matt 7:12">were he in a like case</ref>. [18] And if he should fall into some other venial sin, let him confess to his brother priest. [19] And if there be no priest there, let him confess to his brother, until he shall have a priest who may absolve him canonically, as has been said. [20] And let these have absolutely no power to enjoin any other penance save this: <ref to="John 8:11">Go, and sin no more</ref>.
[21] Keep this writing with you until Pentecost, that it may be the better observed; there you shall be with your brothers. [22] And these things, and all others that are lacking in the rule, with the help of the Lord God you shall see fulfilled.
</p>

## A Letter Sent to the Whole Order <a id="epord"></a>

<p id="epord-1">
[1] In the name of the sovereign Trinity and the holy Unity of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.
[2] To all the reverend brothers most worthy of love, to Brother A., general minister of the religion of the Lesser Brothers, his lord, and to the other general ministers who shall come after him, and to all the ministers and custodians and priests of that same brotherhood, humble in Christ, and to all the simple and obedient brothers, the first and the last, [3] Brother Francis, a man vile and frail, your little servant, <ref to="Rev 1:5">sends greeting in him who has redeemed and washed us in his most precious blood</ref>, [4] <ref to="Ezra 8:6">at whose name, all you who hear, adore him with fear and reverence, bowed low upon the earth</ref>: the Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="Luke 1:32">the Son of the Most High, is his name</ref>, <ref to="Rom 1:25">who is blessed forever</ref>.
[5] Hear, my lords, my sons and brothers, <ref to="Acts 2:14">and give ear to my words</ref>. [6] <ref to="Isa 55:3">Bow down the ear</ref> of your heart and obey the voice of the Son of God. [7] Keep his commandments with your whole heart and fulfill his counsels with a perfect mind. [8] <ref to="Ps 135:1">Give praise to him, for he is good</ref><ref to="Tob 13:6">and exalt him in your works</ref>; [9] <ref to="Tob 13:4">for this is why he has sent you</ref> into the whole world, that by word and deed you may bear witness to his voice and make all men know <ref to="Tob 13:4">that there is no Almighty save him</ref>. [10] <ref to="Heb 12:7">Persevere in discipline and holy obedience</ref>, and with good and firm resolve fulfill what you have promised him. [11] <ref to="Heb 12:7">The Lord God offers himself to us as to sons</ref>.
[12] I therefore beseech all of you, brothers, with a kiss upon your feet and with all the love of which I am capable, that you show all reverence and all honor, as far as ever you can, to the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, [13] in whom the things that are in heaven and the things that are on earth <ref to="Col 1:20">have been made peaceful and reconciled to almighty God</ref>.
[14] I also entreat in the Lord all my brothers who are priests, or shall be, or desire to be priests of the Most High, that whenever they wish to celebrate Mass, being themselves pure, they perform purely and with reverence the true sacrifice of the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, with holy and clean intention, not for any earthly thing nor out of fear or love of any man, <ref to="Eph 6:6; Col 3:22">as if to please men</ref>; [15] but let the whole will, so far as grace assists, be directed toward God, desiring thereby to please the supreme Lord alone, for he alone works there according as it pleases him; [16] since, as he himself says: <ref to="Luke 22:19; 1 Cor 11:24">Do this in remembrance of me</ref>; and if anyone does otherwise, <ref to="1 Cor 11:27">he becomes another Judas the traitor and is made guilty of the body and blood of the Lord</ref>.
[17] Remember, my brother priests, what is written of the law of Moses, <ref to="Heb 10:28">how one who transgressed it, even in bodily matters, died without any mercy by the sentence of the Lord</ref>. [18] How much greater and worse torments does he deserve to suffer who has trodden underfoot the Son of God and counted the blood of the covenant unclean, by which he was sanctified, <ref to="Heb 10:29">and has offered insult to the Spirit of grace</ref>? [19] For a man despises, defiles, and treads underfoot the Lamb of God when, as the Apostle says, <ref to="1 Cor 11:29">not discerning</ref> and distinguishing the holy bread of Christ from other foods or works, he either eats it unworthily, or even, though he be worthy, eats it emptily and unworthily, since the Lord says through the prophet: Cursed be the man <ref to="Jer 48:10">who does the work of God fraudulently</ref>. [20] And priests who refuse to lay this to heart he condemns in truth, saying: <ref to="Mal 2:2">I will curse your blessings</ref>.
[21] Hear, my brothers: if the blessed Virgin is so honored, as is fitting, because she bore him in her most holy womb; if the blessed Baptist trembled and dared not touch the sacred head of God; if the sepulcher in which he lay for a time is venerated, [22] how holy, just, and worthy ought that man to be who handles with his hands, receives with heart and mouth, and offers to others to receive, him who is now no longer to die but to live and be glorified forever, <ref to="1 Pet 1:12">upon whom the angels desire to gaze</ref>!
[23] See your dignity, <ref to="1 Cor 1:26">brother</ref> priests, and be holy, <ref to="Lev 19:2">for he is holy</ref>. [24] And just as the Lord God has honored you above all men for the sake of this ministry, so too do you above all men love, revere, and honor him. [25] It is a great misery and a pitiable weakness that, when you have him thus present, you should care for anything else in all the world. [26] Let the whole man be seized with awe, let the whole world tremble, and let heaven exult, when upon the altar in the hand of the priest is Christ, <ref to="John 11:27">the Son of the living God</ref>! [27] O wondrous height and astounding condescension! O sublime humility! O humble sublimity, that the Lord of all things, God and the Son of God, so humbles himself that for our salvation he hides himself under the little form of bread! [28] See, brothers, <ref to="Ps 61:9">the humility of God, and pour out your hearts before him</ref>; and humble yourselves also, <ref to="1 Pet 5:6; Jas 4:10">that you may be exalted by him</ref>. [29] Keep back therefore nothing of yourselves for yourselves, that he who gives himself wholly to you may receive you wholly.
[30] I admonish and exhort in the Lord, therefore, that in the places where the brothers dwell only one Mass be celebrated each day according to the form of holy Church. [31] But if there be several priests in a place, let one, for the love of charity, be content to hear the celebration of the other priest; [32] for the Lord Jesus Christ fills those who are worthy of him, whether present or absent. [33] He, though he seem to be in many places, yet remains undivided and suffers no diminishment, but, one everywhere, works as it pleases him, together with the Lord God the Father and the Holy Spirit the Paraclete, forever and ever. Amen.
[34] And, <ref to="John 8:47">because he who is of God hears the words of God</ref>, we therefore who are more especially appointed to the divine offices ought not only to hear and do what God says, but also, to impress upon ourselves the loftiness of our Creator and our subjection to him, to guard the vessels and other appointed things that contain his holy words. [35] Therefore I admonish all my brothers and encourage them in Christ that, wherever they find the divine words written, they venerate them as best they can; [36] and, as far as it concerns them, if they are not well kept or lie dishonorably scattered in any place, let them gather them up and set them in order, honoring the Lord in the words <ref to="3 Kgs 2:4">which he has spoken</ref>. [37] <ref to="1 Tim 4:5">For many things are sanctified by the words of God</ref>, and by the power of the words of Christ the sacrament of the altar is confected.
[38] Moreover I confess to the Lord God, the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, to blessed Mary ever Virgin and to all the saints in heaven and on earth, to Brother H., minister of our religion, as to my venerable lord, and to the priests of our order and to all my other blessed brothers, all my sins. [39] In many things have I offended by my grievous fault, especially that I have not kept the rule which I promised the Lord, nor said the office as the rule prescribes, whether through negligence, or on occasion of my infirmity, or because I am ignorant and unlettered. [40] And so, by all means, I pray as I can Brother H., my lord the general minister, that he cause the rule to be inviolably observed by all; [41] and that the clerics say the office with devotion before God, attending not to the melody of the voice but to the harmony of the mind, so that the voice may accord with the mind, and the mind indeed accord with God, [42] that through purity of heart they may be able to please God and not by the wantonness of the voice caress the ears of the people. [43] For I promise firmly to keep these things, as God shall give me grace; and I shall hand them on to be observed by the brothers who are with me, in the office and in the other things ordained by the rule.
[44] But whichever of the brothers refuse to observe these things, I do not hold them to be Catholics nor my brothers; nor do I even wish to see them or speak with them, until they have done penance. [45] I say this also of all others who go wandering about, having set aside the discipline of the rule; [46] for our Lord Jesus Christ gave up his life <ref to="Phil 2:8">rather than fail in obedience to his most holy Father</ref>.
[47] I, Brother Francis, a useless man and an unworthy creature of the Lord God, say through the Lord Jesus Christ to Brother H., minister of our whole religion, and to all the general ministers who shall come after him, and to the other custodians and guardians of the brothers who are and shall be, that they keep this writing by them, act upon it, and diligently preserve it. [48] And I implore them earnestly to guard the things that are written in it and to cause them to be more carefully observed, according to the good pleasure of almighty God, now and always, as long as this world shall last.
[49] <ref to="Ps 113:23">Blessed are you of the Lord</ref>, you who shall do these things, and may the Lord be with you forever. Amen.
</p>

## A Letter to the Rulers of the Peoples <a id="eprect"></a>

<p id="eprect-1">
[1] To all podestàs and consuls, judges and rulers throughout every land, and to all others into whose hands this letter shall come, Brother Francis, your little and despised servant in the Lord God, wishing health and peace to you all.
[2] Consider and see, <ref to="Gen 47:29">for the day of death draws near</ref>. [3] I beg you therefore, with all the reverence I can, that you do not, on account of the cares and anxieties of this world which weigh upon you, consign the Lord to forgetfulness or turn aside from his commandments; for all those who consign him to forgetfulness and turn aside from his commandments <ref to="Ps 118:21">are accursed</ref><ref to="Ezek 33:13"> and shall themselves be consigned to forgetfulness by him</ref>. [4] And when the day of death shall come, everything they supposed themselves to possess <ref to="Luke 8:18">shall be taken away from them</ref>. [5] And the wiser and more powerful they have been in this world, <ref to="Wis 6:7">so much the greater torments shall they endure in hell</ref>. [6] Wherefore I earnestly counsel you, my lords, that, laying aside all care and anxiety, you receive with devotion the most holy body and most holy blood of our Lord Jesus Christ in his holy commemoration. [7] And that you render such honor to the Lord among the people committed to your charge that every evening, by a herald or by some other sign, it may be proclaimed, so that praises and thanks may be offered up by all the people to the almighty Lord God. [8] And if you fail to do this, <ref to="Matt 12:36">know that on the day of judgment you shall be bound to render an account before your Lord God Jesus Christ</ref>.
[9] Those who keep this writing by them and observe it, let them know that they are blessed by the Lord God.
</p>

## Exposition on the Our Father <a id="exppat"></a>

<p id="exppat-1">
[1] O most holy Father: our creator, redeemer, comforter, and savior.
[2] Who art in heaven: in the angels and in the saints; illuminating them unto knowledge, for thou, Lord, art light; enkindling them unto love, for thou, Lord, art love; dwelling within them and filling them unto blessedness, for thou, Lord, art the supreme good, the eternal good, from whom comes every good, without whom there is no good.
[3] Hallowed be thy name: may the knowledge of thee grow bright in us, that we may know <ref to="Eph 3:18">what is the breadth </ref> of thy benefits, the length of thy promises, the height of thy majesty, and the depth of thy judgments.
[4] Thy kingdom come: that thou mayest reign in us through grace and cause us to come to thy kingdom, where there is the open vision of thee, the perfect love of thee, the blessed fellowship of thee, and the everlasting enjoyment of thee.
[5] Thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven: <ref to="Luke 10:27">that we may love thee with our whole heart </ref> by ever thinking of thee, with our whole soul by ever longing for thee, with our whole mind by directing all our intentions toward thee, seeking thy honor in all things, and by spending with all our strength all our powers and the senses of soul and body in the service of thy love and in nothing else; and that we may love our neighbors as ourselves, drawing all men as far as we are able to thy love, <ref to="2 Cor 6:3">rejoicing in the good of others as in our own, suffering with them in their misfortunes, and giving offense to no one </ref>.
[6] Our daily bread: thy beloved Son, our Lord Jesus Christ, give us this day: in memory and understanding and reverence of the love which he bore toward us, and of those things which for us he said, did, and endured.
[7] And forgive us our debts: through thy ineffable mercy, through the power of the passion of thy beloved Son, and through the merits and intercession of the most blessed Virgin and of all thine elect.
[8] As we also forgive our debtors: and what we do not fully forgive, do thou, Lord, make us fully forgive, that for thy sake we may truly love our enemies and devoutly intercede for them before thee, <ref to="1 Thess 5:15">rendering to no one evil for evil </ref>, and striving in all things to be of profit in thee.
[9] And lead us not into temptation: whether hidden or manifest, sudden or persistent.
[10] But deliver us from evil: past, present, and to come. Glory be to the Father, etc.
</p>

<p id="formviv-1">

## Fragments of another Rule <a id="fragm"></a>

<p id="fragm-1">
[1] Let us all take heed, brothers, to what the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:44">Love your enemies and do good to those who hate you </ref>, for our Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="1 Pet 2:21">whose footsteps we ought to follow </ref>, called his own betrayer a friend, and of his own accord offered himself to those who crucified him. [2] Therefore our friends are all those who unjustly inflict upon us tribulations, distresses, shame, injuries, sorrows and torments, martyrdom and death; and them we ought to love greatly, for through what they inflict upon us we gain eternal life. [3] And let us chastise our body, crucifying it together with its vices, its lusts, and its sins, for by living according to the flesh it seeks to strip us of the love of Jesus Christ and of eternal life, and to cast itself, along with the soul, into hell; [4] for by our own fault we were foul, opposed to the good, and prompt and eager toward evil; [5] for as the Lord says: <ref to="Mark 7:21">«From the heart proceed and come forth evil thoughts» </ref> and so on...
[6] But now, since we have forsaken the world, we have nothing else to do but to be diligent in following his will and in pleasing him alone; [7] let us take great care lest we be the ground beside the way, or stony or thorny, according to what the Lord says in the Gospel: <ref to="Luke 8:11">«The seed is the word of God </ref>; [8] but that which fell beside the way, <ref to="Luke 8:5">was trodden underfoot» </ref> and so on up to where it says: <ref to="Luke 8:15">«they bring forth fruit in patience» </ref>.
[9] And therefore, all you brothers, as the Lord says: «Let us leave the dead... <ref to="Matt 8:22">to bury their own» </ref>; [10] and let us take great care against the malice and cunning of Satan, who wills that a man should not have his strength and his heart turned toward the Lord God; [11] and, prowling about, he longs, under the guise of some reward or assistance, to seize the heart of man and to smother the word and commandments of the Lord out of his memory, wishing to inhabit and blind the heart of man through worldly affairs and cares, as the Lord says: [12] <ref to="Matt 12:43">«When the unclean spirit» </ref> and so on up to where it says: [13] <ref to="Matt 12:45">«The last state of that man becomes worse than the first» </ref>.
[14] Wherefore, all you brothers, let us guard ourselves greatly, lest under the guise of some work or reward or assistance we lose or turn away our mind and heart from the Lord. [15] But in charity, <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God </ref>, I beseech all the brothers, ministers as well as the rest, that with every hindrance removed and every care and anxiety set aside, wherever they can, they may love, serve, and adore the Lord God with a clean heart and a pure mind—for this he seeks above all else. [16] <ref to="John 14:23">And let us always make a dwelling-place and habitation </ref> for him who is the Lord God almighty, Father and Son and Holy Spirit, who said: «Watch therefore, praying at all times, that you may be found worthy to escape all the evils <ref to="Luke 21:36">that are to come, and to stand before the Son of man» </ref>; <ref to="Mark 11:25">«and when you stand to pray </ref>, <ref to="Luke 11:2">say </ref>: <ref to="Matt 6:9">Our Father» </ref>. [17] And let us adore him with a pure heart, <ref to="Luke 18:1">«for we ought always to pray and not to grow faint» </ref>; [18] <ref to="John 4:23">«for the Father too seeks such» worshipers </ref>. [19] «God is spirit, and those who worship him <ref to="John 4:24">must worship him in spirit and in truth» </ref>. <ref to="1 Pet 2:25">And let us turn back to him as «to the shepherd and bishop of our souls» </ref>, who says: <ref to="John 10:11">«I am the good shepherd» </ref> and so on up to where it says: <ref to="John 10:15">«I lay down my life for my sheep» </ref>. [20] <ref to="Matt 23:8-9">«You are all brothers, and call no one on earth your father» </ref> and so on. [21] Nor <ref to="Matt 23:10">«let yourselves be called masters»</ref> and so on. [22] «If you abide in me and my words abide in you, whatever you will, <ref to="John 15:7">you shall ask and it shall be done for you» </ref>. [23] <ref to="Matt 18:20">«Wherever two or three are gathered together in my name» </ref> and so on. [24] <ref to="Matt 28:20">«Behold, I am with you all the days» </ref> and so on. [25] «The words that I have spoken to you <ref to="John 6:64">are spirit and life» </ref>. [26] «I am the way, <ref to="John 14:6">the truth, and the life» </ref>.
[27] Let us therefore hold fast to the words, the teaching, the life, and the holy Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ, who deigned to pray to the Father for us and to make his name known to us, saying: Father, <ref to="John 17:6">«I have manifested your name to men» </ref> and so on up to where it says: [28] «Father, those whom you have given me, I will that where I am, they too may be with me, <ref to="John 17:24">that they may see my glory» </ref><ref to="Matt 20:21">«in your kingdom» </ref>. [29] Glory be to the Father and to the Son and to the Holy Spirit, as it was in the beginning and now and always and unto the ages of ages. Amen.
[30] And let the brothers show to the poor the delight which they have among themselves, as the Apostle says: <ref to="1 John 3:18">«Let us not love in word nor in tongue» </ref> and so on.
[31] Let all the brothers, wherever they are, beware of the evil glance and the frequent company of women, and let none take counsel with them alone. Further on: [32] And let us keep ourselves and all our members very pure, for the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:28">«Everyone who looks upon a woman to lust after her» </ref> and so on. Further on:

[43] Wherefore I entreat, in the charity <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God </ref>, all my brothers who preach, pray, and labor, both clerics and laymen, that they strive to humble themselves in all things, [44] not to boast, not to rejoice in themselves nor inwardly to exalt themselves over good words and deeds, indeed over no good that God does or speaks or works in them and through them at any time, according to what the Lord says: «Nevertheless, do not rejoice in this, <ref to="Luke 10:20">that the spirits are subject to you» </ref>, and so on.

[47] Let all us brothers therefore beware of pride and vainglory; let us guard ourselves greatly against the wisdom of this world and the prudence of the flesh. [48] For the spirit of the flesh wills and strives greatly for having words, but little for the doing of them; [49] and it seeks not the inner religion and holiness of the spirit, but wills and desires a religion and holiness that appears outwardly to men. [50] And these are they of whom the Lord says: «Amen I say to you, <ref to="Matt 6:2">they have received their reward» </ref>. [51] But the Spirit of the Lord wills that the flesh be mortified and despised, held cheap, abject, and shameful. [52] And it strives after humility and patience, pure simplicity and the true peace of the spirit. [53] And above all it desires the fear of God and the wisdom of God and the divine love of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit.
[54] And let us render all good things to the Lord God most high and supreme, and let us acknowledge that all good things are his. [55] And let him receive all honors and reverences, all praises and blessings, all thanks and glory, to whom belongs every good thing, <ref to="Luke 18:19">who alone is good </ref>.
[56] And when we hear men speak evil of the Lord or blaspheme him, let us for our part speak well, and bless and praise the Lord, <ref to="Rom 1:25">«who is blessed forever» </ref>.
[57] Let us hold all clerics and religious as our masters in those things that pertain to the salvation of the soul, provided they do not depart from my religion; and let us venerate their order and administration and office in the Lord. Further on:
[58] This or a like exhortation or praise all the brothers may proclaim, as far as God inspires them, among all men whatsoever, with the blessing of God and the permission of their minister: [59] Fear and honor, praise and bless, <ref to="1 Thess 5:18">give thanks </ref> and adore the Lord our God, almighty in Trinity and Unity, Father and Son and Holy Spirit, creator of all things. [60] <ref to="Matt 3:2">Do penance </ref><ref to="Luke 3:8">and bring forth fruits worthy of penance </ref>, for know that we shall soon die. [61] <ref to="Luke 6:38">«Give, and it shall be given to you» </ref>. [62] <ref to="Luke 6:37">Forgive, and it shall be forgiven you </ref>. [63] And <ref to="Matt 6:14">«if you do not forgive» </ref>, <ref to="Mark 11:25">the Lord will not «forgive you your sins» </ref>; <ref to="Jas 5:16">confess all your sins </ref>. [68] And whoever comes to them, friend or adversary, let them in no way contend with them. [69] And wherever the brothers are, and in whatever place they find themselves, <ref to="1 Pet 4:9">they ought spiritually and diligently to look after and honor one another «without murmuring» </ref>. ## The Office of the Lord's Passion <a id="offpass"></a>

<aside>
[Introduction]
</aside>

<p id="offpass-1">
Here begin the psalms which our most blessed father Francis arranged in reverence and memory and praise of the Passion of the Lord. These are to be recited, one at each of the hours of the day and night. And they begin at Compline of Good Friday, because it was on that night that our Lord Jesus Christ was betrayed and seized. And note that blessed Francis recited this office in the following manner: First he said the prayer which the Lord and Master taught us: Most holy Our Father, and so forth, together with the praises, namely: Holy, holy, holy, as is set out above. When the praises with their prayer were finished, he began this antiphon, namely: Holy Mary. First he recited the psalms of holy Mary; afterward he said the other psalms which he had chosen, and at the end of all the psalms that he recited, he said the psalms of the Passion. When the psalm was finished, he said this antiphon, namely: Holy Mary Virgin. When the antiphon was finished, the office was complete.
</p>

</aside>

<p id="regb-1">
[1] This is the Rule and Life of the Friars Minor: namely, to observe the holy Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ by living in obedience, without anything of one's own, and in chastity. [2] Brother Francis promises obedience and reverence to the lord Pope Honorius and to his successors canonically elected, and to the Roman Church. [3] And let the other brothers be bound to obey Brother Francis and his successors.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER II]
Concerning those who wish to embrace this way of life, and how they ought to be received.
</aside>

<p id="regb-2">
[1] If any wish to embrace this life and come to our brothers, let them be sent to their provincial ministers, to whom alone, and to no others, the permission to receive brothers is granted. [2] Let the ministers examine them diligently concerning the catholic faith and the sacraments of the Church. [3] And if they believe all these things and are willing faithfully to confess them and to observe them steadfastly to the end, [4] and if they have no wives, or, if they have, and their wives have already entered a monastery, or have granted them leave by the authority of the diocesan bishop, a vow of continence having already been made, and their wives are of such an age that no suspicion can arise concerning them, [5] <ref to="Matt 19:21"> let them speak to them the word of the holy Gospel </ref>, that they should go and sell all that they have and take care to distribute it to the poor. [6] But if they are unable to do this, their good will suffices. [7] And let the brothers and their ministers take heed not to be anxious about their temporal goods, so that these may freely dispose of their own possessions in whatever way the Lord shall inspire them. [8] If, however, counsel is sought, the ministers may have leave to send them to certain God-fearing persons, by whose advice their goods may be bestowed upon the poor. [9] Afterward let them grant them the garments of probation, namely two tunics without a hood, and a cord, and breeches, and a caperon reaching to the cord, [10] unless it should at some time seem otherwise to those same ministers according to God. [11] But when the year of probation is completed, let them be received into obedience, promising always to observe this life and rule. [12] And in no way shall it be permitted them to leave this religion, according to the command of the lord Pope, [13] <ref to="Luke 9:62"> because, according to the holy Gospel, no one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God </ref>. [14] And those who have already promised obedience may have one tunic with a hood and, if they wish to have it, another without a hood. [15] And those compelled by necessity may wear shoes. [16] And let all the brothers be clothed in poor garments, and they may patch them with sackcloth and other pieces, with the blessing of God. [17] Whom I admonish and exhort not to despise or judge those men whom they see clothed in soft and colored garments, using delicate food and drink, but rather let each one judge and despise himself.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER III]
Concerning the Divine Office and fasting, and how the brothers ought to go through the world.
</aside>

<p id="regb-3">
[1] Let the clerics perform the divine office according to the rite of the holy Roman Church, save for the psalter, [2] for which they may possess breviaries. [3] The lay brothers, however, are to recite twenty-four Our Fathers for matins, five for lauds, and for prime, terce, sext, and none, seven for each of these; for vespers twelve, and for compline seven; [4] and let them pray for the dead. [5] And let them fast from the feast of All Saints until the Nativity of the Lord. [6] But as for the holy Lent that begins at the Epiphany and runs forty continuous days, <ref to="Matt 4:2">which the Lord consecrated by his own holy fast</ref>, those who fast it willingly shall be blessed by the Lord, while those who choose not to are under no obligation. [7] Yet the other Lent, up to the Lord's Resurrection, let them fast. [8] At other times they need not fast except on Fridays. [9] But in a time of manifest necessity the brothers are not bound to bodily fasting. [10] I counsel, admonish, and exhort my brothers in the Lord Jesus Christ that, when they go about the world, <ref to="2 Tim 2:14">they neither quarrel nor contend in words</ref>, nor pass judgment on others; [11] but rather let them be gentle, peaceable, and modest, meek and humble, speaking honorably to all, as is fitting. [12] And they ought not to ride on horseback unless compelled by manifest necessity or infirmity. [13] Into whatever house they enter, let them first say: <ref to="Luke 10:5">Peace to this house</ref>. [14] And according to the holy Gospel, of all the foods that are set before them, <ref to="Luke 10:8">let it be lawful to eat</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER IV]
That the brothers should not receive money.
</aside>

<p id="regb-4">
[1] I firmly command all the brothers that in no way are they to accept coins or money, whether by themselves or through some intermediary. [2] Nevertheless, for the needs of the sick and for clothing the other brothers, let only the ministers and custodians exercise a diligent care through spiritual friends, according to the places and seasons and cold regions, as they shall see to be expedient for necessity; [3] this always being preserved, that, as has been said, they are not to accept coins or money.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER V]
On the manner of working.
</aside>

<p id="regb-5">
[1] Let those brothers to whom the Lord has granted the grace of working labor faithfully and devoutly, [2] in such a way that, banishing the idleness that is the soul's enemy, they do not extinguish the spirit of holy prayer and devotion, to which all other temporal things ought to be subservient. [3] As payment for their labor, let them receive, for themselves and their brothers, the necessities of the body, but not coins or money, [4] and this humbly, as befits the servants of God and the followers of most holy poverty.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER VI]
That the brothers should appropriate nothing to themselves, and concerning the seeking of alms and concerning the sick brothers.
</aside>

<p id="regb-6">
[1] Let the brothers claim nothing for themselves, neither house, nor place, nor anything at all. [2] And <ref to="1 Pet 2:11">as pilgrims and strangers</ref> in this world, serving the Lord in poverty and humility, let them go confidently to seek alms, [3] nor need they be ashamed, <ref to="2 Cor 8:9">for the Lord made himself poor for us in this world</ref>. [4] This is that lofty height of most exalted poverty, which has made you, my dearest brothers, heirs and kings of the kingdom of heaven, has made you poor in possessions and <ref to="Jas 2:5">exalted you in virtues</ref>. [5] Let this be your portion, <ref to="Ps 141:6">which leads into the land of the living</ref>. [6] Cleaving wholly to it, my most beloved brothers, may you desire to have nothing else forever under heaven for the name of our Lord Jesus Christ. [7] And wherever the brothers are and find themselves, let them show themselves as members of one household among one another. [8] And let each one confidently make known his need to another, for <ref to="1 Thess 2:7">if a mother nourishes and loves her child</ref> according to the flesh, how much more diligently ought one to love and nourish his spiritual brother? [9] And if any of them falls into sickness, the other brothers ought to serve him, <ref to="Matt 7:12">just as they would wish to be served themselves</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER VII]
On the penance to be imposed upon brothers who sin.
</aside>

<p id="regb-7">
[1] If any of the friars, at the enemy's prompting, should sin mortally, then—concerning those sins about which it has been decreed among the friars that recourse be had to the provincial ministers alone—these friars are bound to have recourse to them as swiftly as they can, without delay. [2] And the ministers themselves, if they are priests, shall with mercy enjoin a penance upon them; but if they are not priests, they shall have it enjoined through other priests of the order, as shall seem to them, according to God, most fittingly expedient. [3] And they must take care not to grow angry or troubled on account of anyone's sin, for anger and disturbance impede charity both in themselves and in others.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER VIII]
Concerning the election of the general minister of this brotherhood, and concerning the Pentecost chapter.
</aside>

<p id="regb-8">
[1] Let all the brothers be bound always to have as general minister and servant of the whole brotherhood one of the brothers of this religion, and let them be bound to obey him firmly. [2] When he dies, let the election of his successor be made by the provincial ministers and the custodians in the chapter of Pentecost, at which the provincial ministers shall always be bound to assemble together, wherever it shall have been appointed by the general minister; [3] and this once every three years, or at some other interval, greater or lesser, according as it shall have been ordained by the aforesaid minister. [4] And if at any time it should appear to the whole body of provincial ministers and custodians that the aforesaid minister is not adequate for the service and common good of the brothers, the aforesaid brothers, to whom the election is entrusted, shall be bound to elect another for themselves as custodian in the name of the Lord. [5] But after the chapter of Pentecost the ministers and custodians may each, if they wish and it shall seem expedient to them, in that same year summon their brothers once to a chapter in their custodies.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER IX]
Concerning the Preachers.
</aside>

<p id="regb-9">
[1] Let the brothers not preach in the diocese of any bishop when they have been forbidden to do so by him. [2] And let no brother whatsoever dare to preach to the people unless he has been examined and approved by the minister general of this fraternity, and the office of preaching has been granted to him by that same minister. [3] I also admonish and exhort these same brothers that, in the preaching they perform, <ref to="Ps 11:7; Ps 17:31">their words be examined and chaste </ref>, for the benefit and edification of the people, [4] proclaiming to them vices and virtues, punishment and glory with brevity of speech; <ref to="Rom 9:28">for the Lord made a shortened word upon the earth </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER X]
On the admonition and correction of the brothers.
</aside>

<p id="regb-10">
[1] Those brothers who are ministers and servants of the other brothers shall visit and admonish their brothers, and correct them humbly and charitably, commanding them nothing that would be against their souls and against our Rule. [2] But those brothers who are subjects should remember that for God's sake they have renounced their own wills. [3] Wherefore I firmly command them to obey their ministers in all things that they have promised the Lord to observe and that are not contrary to their souls and to our Rule. [4] And wherever there are brothers who know and recognize that they cannot observe the Rule spiritually, they ought to and may have recourse to their ministers. [5] Let the ministers receive them charitably and kindly, and show them such familiarity that they may speak and act toward them as masters toward their servants; [6] for so it ought to be, that the ministers be the servants of all the brothers. [7] I admonish and exhort in the Lord Jesus Christ that the brothers beware of all pride, vainglory, envy, <ref to="Luke 12:15">avarice </ref>, <ref to="Matt 13:22">the care and anxiety of this world </ref>, detraction and murmuring; and let those who are unlettered not be concerned to learn letters. [8] But let them attend to this: that above all things they ought to desire to have the Spirit of the Lord and his holy operation, [9] to pray always to him with a pure heart, and to keep humility, patience in persecution and in sickness, [10] and to love those who persecute us and rebuke and reproach us, for the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:44">Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute and slander you </ref>. [11] Blessed are they who suffer persecution for the sake of justice, <ref to="Matt 5:10">for theirs is the kingdom of heaven </ref>. [12] <ref to="Matt 10:22">But whoever perseveres to the end shall be saved </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER XI]
That the brothers should not enter the monasteries of nuns.
</aside>

<p id="regb-11">
[1] I firmly command all the brothers that they hold no suspect fellowship or private counsel with women, [2] and that they not enter the monasteries of nuns, save only those to whom special leave has been granted by the Apostolic See; [3] nor let them become godfathers to men or women, lest on this occasion scandal arise among the brothers or concerning the brothers.
</p>

<aside>
[CHAPTER XII] On those who go among the Saracens and other unbelievers.
</aside>

<p id="regb-12">
[1] Any of the brothers who by divine inspiration should wish to go among the Saracens and other unbelievers must seek leave for this from their provincial ministers. [2] But the ministers are to grant leave to go to none save those whom they judge fit to be sent. [3] Beyond this, I enjoin upon the ministers by obedience that they petition the lord Pope for one of the cardinals of the holy Roman Church, who is to be the governor, protector, and corrector of this brotherhood, [4] <ref to="Col 1:23"> so that, ever submissive and subject at the feet of that same holy Church, steadfast in the catholic faith, </ref> we may observe the poverty and humility and the holy Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ, which we have firmly promised.
</p>

## The Rule Without a Papal Bull <a id="regnb"></a>

<aside>
[Prologue]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-1">
[1] In the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit! [2] This is the life of the Gospel of Jesus Christ, which Brother Francis asked the Lord Pope to grant and confirm to him; and he granted and confirmed it to him and to his brothers, both present and to come. [3] Brother Francis, and whoever shall be head of this religious order, is to promise obedience and reverence to the Lord Pope Innocent and to his successors. [4] And all the other brothers are bound to obey Brother Francis and his successors.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter I: That the brothers ought to live without property, and in chastity and obedience]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-2">
[1] This is the rule and life of these brothers: namely, to live in obedience, in chastity, and without property of their own, and to follow the teaching and footsteps of our Lord Jesus Christ, who says: [2] "If you would be perfect, <ref to="Matt 19:21">go </ref><ref to="Luke 18:22"> and sell all </ref> that you have, and give to the poor, and you shall have treasure in heaven; and come, <ref to="Matt 19:21">follow me." </ref> [3] And: "If anyone wishes to come after me, <ref to="Matt 16:24">let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me." </ref> [4] Likewise: "If anyone wishes to come to me and does not hate father and mother and wife and children and brothers and sisters, and even his own soul besides, <ref to="Luke 14:26">he cannot be my disciple." </ref> [5] And: "Everyone who leaves father or mother, brothers or sisters, wife or children, houses or fields for my sake, <ref to="Matt 19:29; Mark 10:29; Luke 18:29">shall receive a hundredfold and shall possess eternal life." </ref>
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter II: On the Reception and Clothing of the Brothers]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-3">
[1] If anyone, moved by divine inspiration and desiring to embrace this way of life, comes to our brothers, let him be received kindly by them. [2] And if he is resolved to accept our life, let the brothers take great care not to concern themselves with his temporal affairs, but let them present him to their minister as soon as they can. [3] The minister, for his part, should receive him kindly and encourage him, and diligently set forth to him the tenor of our life. [4] This done, the aforesaid man, if he wishes and is able to do so spiritually and without hindrance, should sell all his goods and take pains to distribute them wholly to the poor. [5] Let the brothers and the minister of the brothers take care not to concern themselves in any way with his affairs; [6] and let them not receive any money, neither by themselves nor through an intermediary. [7] If nonetheless they are in need, the brothers may, out of necessity, receive other things necessary for the body apart from money, just as other poor people do. [8] And when he has returned, let the minister grant him the clothing of probation for the space of one year, namely two tunics without a hood, a cord, breeches, and a caperon reaching to the cord. [9] When the year and term of probation is finished, let him be received into obedience. [10] Thereafter it will not be lawful for him to go over to another religious order, nor to «wander outside obedience,» according to the command of the lord Pope and in accordance with the Gospel; <ref to="Luke 9:62">for «no one who puts his hand to the plough and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God.»</ref> [11] But if anyone comes who cannot give away his goods without hindrance yet has the spiritual will to do so, let him leave them, and that suffices for him. [12] Let no one be received contrary to the form and institution of the holy Church.
[13] The other brothers, who have promised obedience, may have one tunic with a hood and another without a hood, if it be necessary, and a cord and breeches. [14] And let all the brothers be clothed in poor garments, and they may patch them with sackcloth and other pieces, with God's blessing; for the Lord says in the Gospel: <ref to="Luke 7:25">«Those who are in costly raiment and live in luxury»</ref> and «those who are clothed in soft garments <ref to="Matt 11:8">are in the houses of kings.»</ref> [15] And though they be called hypocrites, let them nonetheless not cease to do good, nor seek costly garments in this world, that they may be able to have raiment in the kingdom of heaven.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter III: On the Divine Office and Fasting]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-4">
[1] The Lord says: <ref to="Mark 9:28">This kind of demons cannot be cast out except by fasting and prayer</ref>; [2] and again: <ref to="Matt 6:16">"When you fast, do not become gloomy like the hypocrites"</ref>. [3] For this reason let all the brothers, whether clerics or laymen, perform the divine office, the praises and the prayers, according to what they are bound to do. [4] Let the clerics perform the office and recite it for the living and for the dead according to the custom of clerics. [5] <ref to="Ps 50">And for the failings and negligence of the brothers let them say every day the Miserere mei Deus</ref> with the Our Father; [6] <ref to="Ps 129">and for the departed brothers let them say the De profundis</ref> with the Our Father. [7] And let them have only such books as are necessary to fulfill their office. [8] And to the laymen also who know how to read the psalter it shall be permitted to have it. [9] But to the others who do not know their letters it shall not be permitted to have a book. [10] Let the laymen say the Credo in Deum and twenty-four Our Fathers with the Gloria Patri for matins; but for lauds five; for prime the Credo in Deum and seven Our Fathers with the Gloria Patri; for terce, sext, and none, and for each of these hours, seven; for vespers twelve; for compline the Credo in Deum and seven Our Fathers with the Gloria Patri; for the dead seven Our Fathers with the Requiem aeternam; and for the failings and negligence of the brothers three Our Fathers every day.
[11] And likewise let all the brothers fast from the feast of All Saints until Christmas, and from the Epiphany, when our Lord Jesus Christ began to fast, until Easter. [12] But at other times, according to this way of life, they shall not be bound to fast except on Fridays. [13] And it shall be lawful for them to eat of all the foods that are set before them, <ref to="Luke 10:8">according to the Gospel</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter IV: On the ministers and other brothers, how they are to be appointed]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-5">
[1] In the name of the Lord! [2] Let all the brothers who are appointed ministers and servants of the other brothers, in the provinces and in the places where they may be, station their brothers, whom they should often visit and spiritually admonish and comfort. [3] And let all my other blessed brothers diligently obey them in those things that pertain to the salvation of the soul and are not contrary to our way of life. [4] And let them act among themselves as the Lord says: "Whatever you wish <ref to="Matt 7:12">that men should do to you, do you also to them"</ref>; [5] and: "What you would not have done to yourself, do not do to another." [6] And let the ministers and servants remember what the Lord says: I came not to be ministered unto, <ref to="Matt 20:28">but to minister</ref>, and that the care of the souls of the brothers has been entrusted to them; concerning these souls, if any should be lost through their fault and bad example, <ref to="Matt 12:36">on the day of judgment they will have to render an account</ref> before the Lord Jesus Christ.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter V: On the correction of the brothers when they give offense]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-6">
[1] Therefore guard your own souls and the souls of your brothers; <ref to="Heb 10:31">for it is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God</ref>. [2] But if any of the ministers should command one of the brothers something contrary to our way of life or against his own soul, he is not bound to obey him; for that is no obedience in which a fault or sin is committed. [3] Nevertheless, let all the brothers who are under the ministers and servants weigh the deeds of the ministers and servants reasonably and carefully. [4] And if they see any one of them walking according to the flesh and not according to the spirit, in a manner opposed to the uprightness of our life, then, after the third admonition, if he has not amended himself, let them report him at the Chapter of Pentecost to the minister and servant of the whole brotherhood, with no opposition to hinder them. [5] But if anywhere among the brothers there is some brother who wishes to walk according to the flesh and not according to the spirit, let the brothers with whom he is admonish him, instruct him, and correct him humbly and diligently. [6] And if, after the third admonition, he will not amend himself, let them, as quickly as they can, send him or make him known to his minister and servant, and let that minister and servant deal with him as shall seem to him, according to God, best and most fitting.
[7] And let all the brothers, ministers and servants as well as the rest, take care not to be disturbed or angered on account of another's sin or evil, for the devil desires, through the fault of one, to corrupt many; [8] but let them spiritually, as best they can, help the one who has sinned, for it is not the healthy who have need of a physician, <ref to="Matt 9:12; Mark 2:17">but those who are ill</ref>.
[9] Likewise, let none of the brothers hold power or dominion in this matter, above all among themselves. [10] For as the Lord says in the Gospel: «The princes of the nations lord it over them, <ref to="Matt 20:25">and those who are greater exercise power over them»</ref>, <ref to="Matt 20:26">it shall not be so among the brothers</ref>. [11] And whoever wishes to become greater among them, <ref to="Matt 20:26">let him be their minister</ref> and servant. [12] <ref to="Luke 22:26">And let him who is greater among them become as the lesser</ref>.
[13] Nor let any brother do evil or speak evil to another; [14] <ref to="Gal 5:13">rather, through the charity of the spirit, let them willingly serve and obey one another</ref>. [15] And this is the true and holy obedience of our Lord Jesus Christ. [16] And all the brothers, as often as they turn aside from the commandments of the Lord and wander outside obedience, <ref to="Ps 118:21">as the prophet says</ref>, let them know that they are cursed and outside obedience so long as they knowingly persist in such sin. [17] And when they persevere in the commandments of the Lord, which they have promised through the holy Gospel and their own way of life, let them know that they stand in true obedience, and let them be blessed by the Lord.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VI: On the recourse of the brothers to their ministers, and that no brother should be called prior]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-7">
[1] Wherever the brothers may be, if they cannot observe our way of life, let them return as quickly as they can to their minister, making this known to him. [2] And let the minister be diligent to provide for them just as he himself would wish to be treated, <ref to="Matt 7:12">were he in a like situation</ref>. [3] And let no one be called prior, but let all alike be called lesser brothers. [4] <ref to="John 13:14">And let each one wash the feet of the other</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VII: On the Manner of Serving and Working]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-8">
[1] Wherever the brothers may be, dwelling among others to serve or to labor, let them not be stewards, nor chancellors, nor take charge in the houses in which they serve; nor let them accept any office <ref to="Mark 8:36">that might breed scandal or work harm to their own soul</ref>; [2] but let them be lesser and subject to all who are in that same house.
[3] And let the brothers who know how to work labor and practice the very craft they have learned, provided it be not contrary to the soul's salvation and can be pursued honorably. [4] For the prophet says: "You shall eat the fruits of your labors; <ref to="Ps 127:2">blessed are you, and it shall be well with you"</ref><var psalter="R"></var>; [5] and the Apostle: <ref to="2 Thess 3:10">He who will not work, let him not eat</ref>; [6] and: Let each one <ref to="1 Cor 7:24">remain</ref> in that craft and office in which he was called. [7] And in return for their labor they may receive all things necessary, save money. [8] And when there is need, let them go for alms like the other poor. [9] And it is permitted them to have the tools and instruments suited to their crafts.
[10] Let all the brothers strive to devote themselves to good works, for it is written: Always do something good, that the devil may find you occupied. [11] And again: Idleness is the enemy of the soul. [12] Therefore the servants of God ought always to apply themselves to prayer or to some good work.
[13] Let the brothers take heed, wherever they may be, in hermitages or in other places, that they claim no place as their own nor withhold it from anyone. [14] And whoever comes to them, whether friend or foe, thief or robber, let him be received kindly. [15] And wherever the brothers are, in whatever place they find themselves, <ref to="1 Pet 4:9">let them spiritually and diligently see one another again and honor one another without murmuring</ref>. [16] And let them take heed that they show themselves not outwardly sad and gloomy hypocrites; <ref to="Phil 4:4">but let them show themselves rejoicing in the Lord</ref>, cheerful, and becomingly gracious.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter VIII: That the brothers should not receive money]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-9">
[1] The Lord commands in the Gospel: See, <ref to="Luke 12:15">beware of all malice and avarice</ref>; [2] and: <ref to="Luke 21:34">Take heed to yourselves against the anxiety of this world and the cares of this life</ref>. [3] Therefore let no brother, wherever he may be and wherever he may go, in any way take up, receive, or cause to be received money or coins, neither on the pretext of clothing nor of books nor as the price of any labor—indeed, on no pretext whatsoever—save only for the manifest necessity of sick brothers; for we ought not to hold or reckon money and coins to be of any greater use than stones. [4] And the devil seeks to blind those who covet it or reckon it better than stones. [5] Let us therefore beware, <ref to="Matt 19:27">we who have left all things</ref>, lest for so trifling a thing we lose the kingdom of heaven. [6] And if in any place we should find coins, let us care no more for them than for the dust we trample beneath our feet, <ref to="Eccl 1:2">for it is vanity of vanities, and all is vanity</ref>. [7] And if perchance—which God forbid—it should happen that some brother collects or possesses money or coins, save only for the aforesaid necessity of the sick, let all of us brothers hold him to be <ref to="John 12:6">a false brother, an apostate, a thief, a robber, and a keeper of the money-bag</ref>, unless he truly repents. [8] And in no way let the brothers receive, or cause to be received, or seek, or cause to be sought money for alms, nor coins for any houses or dwellings; nor let them go with any person who is seeking money or coins for such dwellings. [9] But other services, which are not contrary to our way of life, the brothers may render to such places with the blessing of God. [10] The brothers may, however, in the manifest necessity of lepers, seek alms for them. [11] Yet let them beware greatly of money. [12] Likewise let all the brothers beware, lest for the sake of any base gain they roam about the lands.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter IX: On Begging for Alms]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-10">
[1] Let all the brothers strive to follow the humility and poverty of our Lord Jesus Christ, and let them remember that we ought to possess nothing else of this whole world except, as the Apostle says, having food and something to cover us, <ref to="1 Tim 6:8">with these we are content</ref>. [2] And they ought to rejoice when they dwell among lowly and despised persons, among the poor and the weak and the sick and the lepers and those who beg by the roadside. [3] And when it is necessary, let them go for alms. [4] And let them not be ashamed, but rather remember that our Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="John 11:27">the Son of the living God</ref> almighty, <ref to="Isa 50:7">set his face like the hardest stone</ref>, and was not ashamed. [5] He was poor and a guest and lived on alms, both he and the blessed Virgin and his disciples. [6] And when people put them to shame and refuse to give them alms, let them give thanks to God for it; for from these humiliations they shall receive great honor before the tribunal of our Lord Jesus Christ. [7] And let them know that the shame is reckoned not to those who suffer it but to those who inflict it. [8] Alms are an inheritance and a right owed to the poor, which our Lord Jesus Christ acquired for us. [9] And the brothers who labor in gathering them shall have a great reward, and they cause those who give to earn and gain; for all that people leave behind in the world shall perish, but for the charity and the alms they have given they shall have a reward from the Lord. [10] And let each one confidently make known his need to another, so that the other may find and supply for him what is necessary. [11] And let each one love and nourish his brother, <ref to="1 Thess 2:7">as a mother loves and nourishes her son</ref>, in those matters in which God shall grant him grace. [12] And let him who does not eat <ref to="Rom 14:3">not judge him who eats</ref>. [13] And whenever necessity shall arise, let it be lawful for all the brothers, wherever they may be, to make use of all the foods that people are able to eat, just as the Lord says of David, <ref to="Matt 12:4">who ate the loaves of proposition</ref>, <ref to="Mark 2:26">which it was not lawful to eat except for the priests</ref>. [14] And let them remember what the Lord says: But take heed to yourselves, lest perhaps your hearts be weighed down with surfeiting and drunkenness and the cares of this life, and that day come upon you suddenly; [15] for it shall come as a snare upon all <ref to="Luke 21:34-35">who sit upon the face of the whole earth</ref>. [16] Likewise also, in a time of manifest necessity, let all the brothers provide for their needs as the Lord shall grant them grace, for necessity has no law.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter X: On the sick brothers]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-11">
[1] Should any of the brothers fall ill, wherever he may be, the other brothers must not abandon him, but shall appoint one brother, or several if need be, to attend upon him, <ref to="Matt 7:12">just as they would wish to be attended themselves</ref>; [2] yet in a case of the utmost necessity they may entrust him to some person who is bound to see to his infirmity. [3] And I beg the sick brother to render thanks for all things to the Creator, and to desire to be such as the Lord wills him to be, whether in health or in sickness; for all those <ref to="Acts 13:48">whom God has foreordained to eternal life</ref> He instructs with the goads of scourges and infirmities and with the spirit of compunction, as the Lord says: <ref to="Rev 3:19">"Those whom I love I rebuke and chastise."</ref> [4] But if anyone should be troubled or grow angry, whether against God or against the brothers, or should perhaps anxiously demand remedies, too eager to free the flesh—which is soon to die and is the enemy of the soul—it comes to him from evil and is carnal, and he does not seem to be one of the brothers, since he loves the body more than the soul.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XI: That the brothers should neither blaspheme nor slander, but should love one another]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-12">
[1] And let all the brothers take heed of themselves, <ref to="2 Tim 2:14">that they neither slander nor contend in words</ref>; [2] rather let them strive to keep silence whenever God bestows His grace upon them. [3] Let them not quarrel among themselves nor with others, but let them endeavor to answer humbly, saying: <ref to="Luke 17:10">I am an unprofitable servant</ref>. [4] And let them not grow angry, for everyone who is angry with his brother shall be liable to judgment; whoever says to his brother, 'Raca,' shall be liable to the council; whoever says, 'You fool,' <ref to="Matt 5:22">shall be liable to the fire of hell</ref>. [5] And let them love one another, as the Lord says: 'This is my commandment, that you love one another, <ref to="John 15:12">as I have loved you'</ref>. [6] <ref to="Jas 2:18">And let them show by their works</ref> the love they bear to one another, as the Apostle says: 'Let us not love in word nor in tongue, <ref to="1 John 3:18">but in deed and in truth'</ref>. [7] <ref to="Titus 3:2">And let them blaspheme no one</ref>. [8] Let them not murmur, let them not disparage others, for it is written: <ref to="Rom 1:29">Whisperers and backbiters are hateful to God</ref>. [9] <ref to="Titus 3:2">And let them be modest, showing all gentleness toward all men</ref>. [10] Let them not judge, let them not condemn. [11] And as the Lord says, <ref to="Matt 7:3; Luke 6:41">let them not take note of the small sins of others</ref>; [12] <ref to="Isa 38:15">but rather let them reflect upon their own in the bitterness of their soul</ref>. [13] <ref to="Luke 13:24">And let them strive to enter by the narrow gate</ref>, for the Lord says: 'Narrow is the gate and strait is the way that leads to life; and few there are <ref to="Matt 7:14">who find it'</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XII: On the evil of gazing upon and frequenting the company of women]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-13">
[1] Let all the brothers, wherever they are or wherever they go, guard themselves against evil glances and frequent dealings with women. [2] And let no one take counsel with them, nor travel a road alone with them, nor eat from a single dish at table with them. [3] Let priests speak with them honorably, in giving penance or other spiritual counsel. [4] And let no woman whatsoever be received into obedience by any brother; rather, once spiritual counsel has been given to her, let her do penance wherever she wishes. [5] And let us all keep close watch over ourselves and hold all our members pure, for the Lord says: "Whoever looks upon a woman to lust after her <ref to="Matt 5:28">has already committed adultery with her in his heart"</ref>; [6] and the Apostle: Do you not know <ref to="1 Cor 6:19">that your members are the temple of the Holy Spirit?</ref>; and so, whoever violates the temple of God, <ref to="1 Cor 3:17">him shall God destroy</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XIII: On the Avoidance of Fornication]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-14">
[1] If any of the brothers, at the devil's prompting, should commit fornication, let him be stripped of the habit which he has forfeited by his shameful wickedness; let him lay it aside entirely and be utterly cast out from our religion. [2] <ref to="1 Cor 5:4-5"> And afterward let him do penance for his sins </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XIV: How the brothers ought to go about through the world]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-15">
[1] When the brothers go through the world, let them <ref to="Luke 9:3">carry nothing along the way, neither </ref><ref to="Luke 10:4"> purse </ref><ref to="Luke 9:3"> nor bag nor bread nor money </ref><ref to="Matt 10:10"> nor staff </ref>. [2] And into whatever house they enter, let them first say: <ref to="Luke 10:5">Peace to this house </ref>. [3] <ref to="Luke 10:7"> And remaining in that same house, let them eat and drink whatever is set before them </ref>. [4] <ref to="Matt 5:39"> Let them not resist evil </ref>, but to whoever strikes them on one cheek, <ref to="Matt 5:39; Luke 6:29">let them offer the other also </ref>. [5] And to one who takes away their cloak, <ref to="Luke 6:29">let them not refuse even their tunic </ref>. [6] Let them give to everyone who asks of them; and from one who carries off what is theirs, <ref to="Luke 6:30">let them not seek it back again </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XV: That the brothers should not ride on horseback]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-16">
[1] I command all my brothers, both clerics and laymen, whether they journey through the world or dwell in fixed places, that they in no way keep any beast, neither with themselves nor through another nor in any other manner whatsoever. [2] Nor may they be permitted to ride, unless they are compelled by illness or by great necessity.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVI: On Those Who Go Among the Saracens and Other Infidels]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-17">
[1] The Lord says: «Behold, I send you as sheep in the midst of wolves». [2] <ref to="Matt 10:16"> Be therefore wise as serpents and simple as doves </ref>. [3] Wherefore any brother who wishes to go among the Saracens and other unbelievers, let him go with the permission of his minister and servant. [4] And let the minister grant them permission and not refuse it, if he sees them fit to be sent; <ref to="Luke 16:2">for he will be bound to render an account to the Lord </ref>, should he act indiscreetly in this or in other matters. [5] The brothers, moreover, who go, can conduct themselves spiritually among them in two ways. [6] The first way is that they engage in no quarrels or disputes, <ref to="1 Pet 2:13">but be subject to every human creature for God's sake </ref> and confess themselves to be Christians. [7] The other way is that, when they see it to be pleasing to the Lord, they announce the word of God, that men may believe in God the Almighty Father and Son and Holy Spirit, the creator of all things, and in the Son the redeemer and savior, and that they may be baptized and become Christians, since whoever is not born again of water and the Holy Spirit <ref to="John 3:5">cannot enter into the kingdom of God </ref>.
[8] These things and others that shall please the Lord they may speak to them and to others, for the Lord says in the gospel: Everyone who shall confess me before men, I too will confess him before my Father, <ref to="Matt 10:32">who is in heaven </ref>. [9] And: Whoever shall be ashamed of me and of my words, the Son of Man also shall be ashamed of him, <ref to="Luke 9:26">when he shall come in his majesty and that of the Father and of the angels </ref>.
[10] And let all the brothers, wherever they are, remember that they have given themselves and surrendered their bodies to the Lord Jesus Christ. [11] And for love of him they ought to expose themselves to enemies both visible and invisible; for the Lord says: Whoever shall lose his soul for my sake, <ref to="Luke 9:24">shall save it </ref><ref to="Matt 25:46"> unto life eternal </ref>. [12] Blessed are they who suffer persecution for justice's sake, <ref to="Matt 5:10">for theirs is the kingdom of heaven </ref>. [13] If they have persecuted me, <ref to="John 15:20">they will also persecute you </ref>. [14] And: If they persecute you in one city, <ref to="Matt 10:23">flee into another </ref>. [15] <ref to="Matt 5:11"> Blessed are you </ref>, <ref to="Luke 6:22">when men shall hate you </ref><ref to="Matt 5:11"> and shall curse you </ref> and <ref to="Luke 6:22">shall persecute you and shall separate you and reproach you and cast out your name as evil </ref><ref to="Matt 5:11"> and when they shall utter all manner of evil against you, lying, for my sake </ref>. [16] <ref to="Luke 6:23"> Rejoice in that day and be glad </ref>, for your reward <ref to="Matt 5:12">is great in heaven </ref>. [17] And I say to you my friends, <ref to="Luke 12:4">be not terrified by these things </ref>, [18] <ref to="Matt 10:28"> and fear not those who kill the body </ref><ref to="Luke 12:4"> and after this have no more that they can do </ref>. [19] See <ref to="Matt 24:6">that you be not troubled </ref>. [20] <ref to="Luke 21:19"> For in your patience you shall possess your souls </ref>; [21] and he who shall persevere unto the end, <ref to="Matt 10:22; Matt 24:13">he shall be saved </ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVII: On Preachers]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-18">
[1] Let no brother preach contrary to the form and institution of the holy Church, nor unless permission has been granted him by his minister. [2] And let the minister take care not to grant it to anyone indiscreetly. [3] Yet let all the brothers preach by their works. [4] And let no minister or preacher claim for himself the ministry of the brothers or the office of preaching, but at whatever hour it is enjoined upon him, let him relinquish his office without any objection.
[5] Wherefore I beseech, in the charity <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God</ref>, all my brothers—preachers, those given to prayer, and laborers, clerics as well as lay—that they strive to humble themselves in all things, [6] not to boast nor to rejoice in themselves nor inwardly to be exalted over good words and works, nor indeed over any good that God at times does or says and works in them and through them, according to what the Lord says: "Nevertheless, do not rejoice in this, <ref to="Luke 10:20">that the spirits are subject to you."</ref> [7] And let us know firmly that nothing belongs to us except vices and sins. [8] And we ought rather to rejoice <ref to="Jas 1:2">when we fall into various temptations</ref> and when we endure whatever anguish of soul or body, or whatever tribulations, in this world for the sake of eternal life.
[9] Let all of us brothers, therefore, beware of all pride and vainglory. [10] <ref to="Rom 8:6">And let us guard ourselves against the wisdom of this world and the prudence of the flesh.</ref> [11] For the spirit of the flesh desires and greatly strives after having words, but little after doing; [12] and it seeks not religion and holiness in the inner spirit, but rather wills and desires to have a religion and holiness that appears outwardly to men. [13] And these are they of whom the Lord says: Amen I say to you, <ref to="Matt 6:2">they have received their reward.</ref> [14] But the Spirit of the Lord wills the flesh to be mortified and despised, lowly and cast down. [15] And it strives after humility and patience and the pure and simple and true peace of the spirit. [16] And always, above all things, it desires divine fear and divine wisdom and the divine love of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.
[17] Let us therefore render all good things to the Lord God, most high and supreme, and acknowledge that all good things are His, and give Him thanks for all, from whom all good things proceed. [18] And may He, the most high and supreme, the only true God, have—and may there be rendered to Him and may He receive—all honors and reverences, all praises and blessings, all thanksgiving and glory, to whom belongs every good, <ref to="Luke 18:19">who alone is good.</ref>
[19] And when we see or hear men speaking or doing evil or blaspheming God, <ref to="Rom 12:21">let us speak well and do well and praise God</ref>, <ref to="Rom 1:25">who is blessed forever.</ref>
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XVIII: How the ministers are to gather together with one another]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-19">
[1] Each year every minister, together with his brothers, may gather wherever it shall please them, on the feast of Saint Michael the Archangel, to treat of those things which pertain to God. [2] For all the ministers who are in the regions beyond the sea and beyond the mountains shall come once every three years, and the other ministers once each year, to the chapter of Pentecost at the church of Saint Mary of the Portiuncula, unless it be ordained otherwise by the minister and servant of the whole brotherhood.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter 19: That the brothers should live as Catholics]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-20">
[1] Let all the brothers be Catholic, and let them live and speak as Catholics. [2] But if any should stray from the Catholic faith and life, whether in word or in deed, and fail to amend himself, let him be utterly cast out from our brotherhood. [3] And let us hold all clerics and all religious as our masters in those things which pertain to the salvation of the soul, so long as they do not depart from our religion, and let us venerate in the Lord their order and office and ministry.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XX: On penance and the reception of the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-21">
[1] And let my blessed brothers, both clerics and lay, confess their sins to the priests of our religion. [2] And if they cannot, let them confess to other discerning and catholic priests, knowing firmly and taking heed that from whatever catholic priests they receive penance and absolution, they will without doubt be absolved of those sins, provided that they endeavor to observe humbly and faithfully the penance enjoined upon them. [3] But if they are then unable to find a priest, let them confess to their brother, as the apostle James says: <ref to="Jas 5:16">«Confess your sins to one another»</ref>. [4] Yet let them not for this reason cease to have recourse to a priest, for the power of binding and loosing is granted to priests alone. [5] And thus, contrite and confessed, let them receive the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ with great humility and veneration, remembering what the Lord says: <ref to="John 6:55">Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood has eternal life</ref>; [6] and: <ref to="Luke 22:19">«Do this in remembrance of me»</ref>.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXI: On the praise and exhortation which all the brothers may offer]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-22">
[1] This exhortation and praise, or one like it, all my brothers may proclaim, whenever it pleases them, among all peoples, with the blessing of God: [2] Fear and honor, praise and bless, <ref to="1 Thess 5:18">give thanks</ref> and adore the Lord God Almighty in Trinity and Unity, Father and Son and Holy Spirit, the Creator of all. [3] <ref to="Matt 3:2">Do penance</ref>, <ref to="Luke 3:8">bring forth fruits worthy of penance</ref>, for we shall soon die. [4] <ref to="Luke 6:38">Give, and it shall be given to you</ref>. [5] <ref to="Luke 6:37">Forgive, and you shall be forgiven</ref>. [6] <ref to="Matt 6:14">And if you do not forgive men their sins</ref>, <ref to="Mark 11:25">the Lord will not forgive you your sins</ref>; <ref to="Jas 5:16">confess all your sins</ref>. [7] Blessed are those who die in penance, for they shall be in the kingdom of heaven. [8] Woe to those who do not die in penance, <ref to="1 John 3:10">for they shall be children of the devil</ref>, <ref to="John 8:41">whose works they do</ref><ref to="Matt 18:8; Matt 25:41"> and they shall go into everlasting fire</ref>. [9] Beware and abstain from all evil, and persevere to the end in what is good.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXII: On the admonition of the brothers]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-23">
[1] Let us all take heed, brothers, to what the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:44">Love your enemies and do good to those who hate you</ref>, [2] for our Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="1 Pet 2:21">whose footsteps we ought to follow</ref>, <ref to="Matt 26:50">called his betrayer his friend</ref> and of his own accord offered himself to those who crucified him. [3] Our friends, therefore, are all those who unjustly inflict upon us tribulations and anguish, shame and injuries, sorrows and torments, martyrdom and death; [4] and them we ought greatly to love, because through what they inflict upon us we gain eternal life.
[5] And let us hold in hatred our own body with its vices and sins; for by living according to the flesh the devil wishes to strip from us the love of Jesus Christ and eternal life, and to bring himself, together with all, to ruin in hell; [6] for through our own fault we are foul, wretched, and opposed to the good, but ready and eager for evil, since, as the Lord says in the Gospel: [7] From the heart proceed and go forth evil thoughts, adulteries, fornications, murders, thefts, avarice, wickedness, deceit, lewdness, an evil eye, false testimonies, blasphemy, <ref to="Mark 7:21-22; Matt 15:19">folly</ref>. [8] <ref to="Mark 7:23">All these evils proceed from within, from the heart of man</ref><ref to="Matt 15:20">and these are the things that defile a man</ref>.
[9] Now, therefore, having forsaken the world, we have nothing else to do but to follow the will of the Lord and to please him. [10] Let us take great care lest we be earth by the wayside, or stony, or thorny ground, according to what the Lord says in the Gospel: [11] <ref to="Luke 8:11">The seed is the word of God</ref>. [12] <ref to="Luke 8:5">That which fell by the wayside and was trodden underfoot</ref>: these are they <ref to="Luke 8:12">who hear</ref><ref to="Matt 13:19">the word and do not understand it</ref>; [13] <ref to="Mark 4:15">and at once</ref><ref to="Luke 8:12">the devil comes</ref><ref to="Matt 13:19">and snatches away</ref><ref to="Mark 4:15">what was sown in their hearts</ref>, and takes the word from their hearts, <ref to="Luke 8:12">lest believing they be saved</ref>. [14] <ref to="Matt 13:20">That which fell upon stony ground</ref>: these are they who, when they have heard the word, <ref to="Mark 4:16">immediately with joy</ref><ref to="Luke 8:13">receive</ref><ref to="Mark 4:16">it</ref>. [15] But when tribulation and persecution arise on account of the word, <ref to="Matt 13:21">they are at once scandalized</ref>, and these have no root in themselves, <ref to="Mark 4:17">but endure only for a time</ref>, <ref to="Luke 8:13">for they believe for a while, and in the time of temptation they fall away</ref>. [16] That which fell among thorns, <ref to="Luke 8:14">these are they</ref><ref to="Mark 4:18">who hear the word of God</ref>, <ref to="Matt 13:22">and the cares</ref><ref to="Mark 4:19">and hardships</ref><ref to="Matt 13:22">of this world and the deceitfulness of riches</ref><ref to="Mark 4:19">and the other desires entering in choke the word and it becomes fruitless</ref>. [17] <ref to="Luke 8:15">But that which was sown upon good ground</ref><ref to="Matt 13:23">was sown</ref>: these are they <ref to="Luke 8:15">who, hearing the word with a good and excellent heart</ref><ref to="Matt 13:23">understand and</ref><ref to="Luke 8:15">retain it and bring forth fruit in patience</ref>. [18] And therefore let us, brothers, as the Lord says, <ref to="Matt 8:22">let the dead bury their own dead</ref>.
[19] And let us take great care against the malice and cunning of Satan, who wishes that a man should not hold his mind and heart toward God. [20] Prowling about, he desires under the guise of some reward or help to take away and choke the word and the commandments of the Lord from the memory, and, wishing to blind a man's heart through worldly affairs and cares, to dwell there, as the Lord says: [21] When the unclean spirit has gone out of a man, <ref to="Matt 12:43">he walks through dry and waterless places seeking rest</ref>; [22] and finding none he says: I will return to my house <ref to="Luke 11:24">from which I came out</ref>. [23] And coming he finds it empty, <ref to="Matt 12:44">swept clean and adorned</ref>. [24] And he goes and takes with him seven other spirits more wicked than himself, and entering in they dwell there, <ref to="Luke 11:26">and the last state of that man becomes worse than the first</ref>.
[25] Wherefore, all brothers, let us guard ourselves greatly, lest under the guise of some reward or work or help we lose or take away our mind and heart from the Lord. [26] But in holy charity, <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God</ref>, I beseech all the brothers, both ministers and the rest, that, every hindrance removed and every care and anxiety set aside, in whatever way they best can, they serve, love, honor, and adore the Lord God with a clean heart and a pure mind, which is what he seeks above all things. [27] <ref to="John 14:23">And let us always make there a dwelling and abode</ref> for him who is the Lord God almighty, Father and Son and Holy Spirit, who says: Watch therefore, praying at all times, that you may be found worthy to escape all the evils <ref to="Luke 21:36">that are to come, and to stand before the Son of Man</ref>. [28] <ref to="Mark 11:25">And when you stand to pray</ref><ref to="Luke 11:2">say</ref>: <ref to="Matt 6:9">Our Father who art in heaven</ref>. [29] And let us adore him with a pure heart, <ref to="Luke 18:1">for we ought always to pray and not to faint</ref>; [30] for the Father seeks such worshippers. [31] God is Spirit, and those who adore him <ref to="John 4:23-24">must adore him in spirit and in truth</ref>. [32] <ref to="1 Pet 2:25">And let us have recourse to him as to the shepherd and bishop of our souls</ref>, who says: I am the good shepherd, who feed my sheep, and for my sheep I lay down my life. [33] You are all brothers; [34] and call no one your father upon earth, for one is your Father, who is in heaven. [35] Neither be called masters; for one is your master, <ref to="Matt 23:8-10">who is in heaven</ref>. [36] If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, whatever you wish <ref to="John 15:7">you shall ask, and it shall be done for you</ref>. [37] Wherever two or three are gathered together in my name, <ref to="Matt 18:20">there am I in the midst of them</ref>. [38] <ref to="Matt 28:20">Behold, I am with you until the consummation of the world</ref>. [39] The words that I have spoken to you <ref to="John 6:64">are spirit and life</ref>. [40] I am the way, <ref to="John 14:6">the truth, and the life</ref>.
[41] Let us therefore hold fast the words, the life, and the teaching and holy Gospel of him who deigned to pray to his Father for us and to make known to us his name, saying: <ref to="John 12:28">Father, glorify thy name</ref>, and glorify thy Son, <ref to="John 17:1">that thy Son may glorify thee</ref>. [42] Father, I have made known thy name to the men <ref to="John 17:6">whom thou gavest me</ref>; for the words which thou gavest me I have given to them; and they received them and knew that I came forth from thee, and they believed that thou didst send me. [43] I pray for them, not for the world, [44] but for those whom thou gavest me, <ref to="John 17:8-10">for they are thine, and all that is mine is thine</ref>. [45] Holy Father, keep in thy name those whom thou gavest me, <ref to="John 17:11">that they may be one, as we also are</ref>. [46] These things I speak in the world, that they may have joy fulfilled in themselves. [47] I have given them thy word; and the world hated them, because they are not of the world, as I also am not of the world. [48] I do not ask that thou take them out of the world, <ref to="John 17:13-15">but that thou keep them from evil</ref>. [49] Sanctify them in truth. [50] Thy word is truth. [51] As thou didst send me into the world, I also have sent them into the world. [52] And for them I sanctify myself, that they too may be sanctified in truth. [53] I do not pray for them alone, but for those <ref to="John 17:17-20">who through their word are to believe in me</ref>, that they may be made perfect in one, and that the world may know that thou didst send me and hast loved them, <ref to="John 17:23">as thou hast loved me</ref>. [54] And I will make known to them thy name, that the love <ref to="John 17:26">with which thou hast loved me may be in them, and I in them</ref>. [55] Father, those whom thou gavest me, I will that where I am, they too may be with me, <ref to="John 17:24">that they may behold thy glory</ref><ref to="Matt 20:21">in thy kingdom</ref>. Amen.
</p>

<aside>
[Chapter XXIII: Prayer and Thanksgiving]
</aside>

<p id="regnb-24">
[1] Almighty, most holy, most high and sovereign God, <ref to="John 17:11">holy Father </ref> and just, <ref to="Matt 11:25">Lord, King of heaven and earth </ref>, for your own sake we give you thanks, <ref to="Gen 1:26; Gen 2:15">because by your holy will and through your only Son with the Holy Spirit you created all things spiritual and corporeal, and, having made us in your image and likeness, you set us in paradise </ref>. [2] And through our own fault we fell. [3] And we give you thanks that, as you created us through your Son, so through your holy love, <ref to="John 17:26">with which you loved us </ref>, you caused him, true God and true man, to be born of the glorious ever-Virgin, most blessed holy Mary, and through his cross and blood and death you willed that we captives be redeemed. [4] And we give you thanks that this same Son of yours is to come in the glory of his majesty, to send the accursed, who did not do penance and did not know you, into the eternal fire, and to say to all who knew you and adored you and served you in penance: Come, blessed of my Father, receive the kingdom <ref to="Matt 25:34">which has been prepared for you from the foundation of the world </ref>.
[5] And because we, all wretched and sinners, are not worthy to name you, we humbly implore that our Lord Jesus Christ, your beloved Son, <ref to="Matt 17:5">in whom you are well pleased </ref>, together with the Holy Spirit the Paraclete, may give you thanks, as it is pleasing to you and to him, for all things — he who ever suffices for you in all things, through whom you have done such great things for us. Alleluia.
[6] And we humbly beseech the glorious mother, most blessed Mary ever-Virgin, blessed Michael, Gabriel and Raphael, and all the choirs of the blessed seraphim, cherubim, thrones, dominations, principalities, <ref to="Col 1:15">powers </ref>, virtues, angels, archangels, blessed John the Baptist, John the Evangelist, Peter, Paul, and the blessed patriarchs, prophets, Innocents, apostles, evangelists, disciples, martyrs, confessors, virgins, blessed Elijah and Enoch, and all the saints who have been and shall be and are — we beseech them, for love of you, that, as it pleases you, they may render you thanks for these things, to you the supreme, true God, eternal and living, <ref to="Rev 19:3">with your most dear Son our Lord Jesus Christ and the Holy Spirit the Paraclete, unto the ages of ages </ref>. Amen. <ref to="Rev 19:4">Alleluia </ref>.
[7] And we, all the Friars Minor, <ref to="Luke 17:10">useless servants </ref>, humbly ask and implore all who wish to serve the Lord God within the holy Catholic and apostolic Church, and all the following orders: priests, deacons, subdeacons, acolytes, exorcists, lectors, doorkeepers, and all clerics, all religious men and women, all lay brothers and little children, the poor and the needy, kings and princes, laborers and farmers, servants and masters, all virgins and the continent and the married, laypeople, male and female, all infants, adolescents, youths and the aged, the healthy and the sick, all the lowly and the great, and all peoples, races, <ref to="Rev 7:9">tribes and tongues </ref>, all nations and all people wherever on earth who are and shall be — that we may all persevere in true faith and penance, for otherwise no one can be saved.
[8] Let us all love the Lord God with our whole heart, with our whole soul, with our whole mind, <ref to="Mark 12:30">with our whole strength </ref> and might, <ref to="Mark 12:33">with our whole understanding </ref>, <ref to="Luke 10:27">with all our powers </ref>, with all our effort, all our affection, all our inmost being, <ref to="Mark 12:30">all our longings and wills </ref> — he who gave and gives to us all our whole body, our whole soul, and our whole life, who created us, <ref to="Tob 13:5">redeemed us, and by his mercy alone will save us </ref>, who has done and does all good things for us, miserable and wretched, rotten and foul, ungrateful and evil as we are.
[9] Let us therefore desire nothing else, wish for nothing else, let nothing else please or delight us but our Creator and Redeemer and Savior, the one true God, who is the fullness of good, all good, the whole good, the true and supreme good, <ref to="Luke 18:19">who alone is good </ref>, loving, gentle, mild and sweet, who alone is holy, just, true, holy and upright, who alone is kind, innocent, pure, <ref to="Rom 11:36">from whom and through whom and in whom </ref> is all pardon, all grace, all glory of all the penitent and the just, of all the blessed rejoicing together in heaven. [10] Let nothing therefore hinder us, nothing separate us, nothing interrupt us. [11] Everywhere, in every place, at every hour and in every season, daily and continually, let us all truly and humbly believe and hold in our hearts and love, honor, adore, serve, praise and bless, glorify and exalt above all, magnify and give thanks to the most high and supreme eternal God, Trinity and Unity, Father and Son and Holy Spirit, the creator of all and the savior of all who believe and hope in him and love him — who, without beginning and without end, is unchangeable, invisible, indescribable, unspeakable, incomprehensible, <ref to="Rom 11:33">unsearchable </ref>, blessed, praiseworthy, glorious, <ref to="Dan 3:52">exalted above all </ref>, sublime, lofty, sweet, lovable, delightful and wholly desirable above all things unto the ages. Amen.
</p>

## A Rule for Hermitages <a id="reger"></a>

<p id="reger-1">
[1] Those who wish to live a life of religion in hermitages should be three brothers, or at most four; let two of them be mothers and have two sons, or at least one. [2] Let these two who are mothers <ref to="Luke 10:38-42">keep to the life of Martha, and let the two sons keep to the life of Mary</ref>, and let them have one enclosure in which each may have his own little cell, where he may pray and sleep. [3] And let them always say Compline of the day immediately after sunset; and let them be zealous to keep silence; and let them say their hours; <ref to="Matt 6:33">and at Matins let them rise and first of all seek the kingdom of God and his righteousness</ref>. [4] And let them say Prime at the hour that is fitting, and after Terce let them break silence; and they may speak and go to their mothers. [5] And, when it pleases them, they may ask alms of them like little poor children, for the love of the Lord God. [6] And afterward let them say Sext and None; and let them say Vespers at the fitting hour. [7] And in the enclosure where they dwell let them not allow any person to enter, nor let them eat there. [8] Let those brothers who are mothers strive to remain apart from every person; and through obedience to their minister let them guard their sons from every person, so that no one may speak with them. [9] And let these sons not speak with any person save with their mothers and with their minister and their custodian, whenever it may please these to visit them with the blessing of the Lord God. [10] But the sons shall at times take up the office of the mothers, according as it shall seem fitting to arrange in turn for a season; and let them strive carefully and zealously to observe all the things said above.
</p>

## A Salutation to the Blessed Virgin Mary <a id="salbmv"></a>

<p id="salbmv-1">
[1] Hail, O Lady, holy Queen, holy Mother of God, Mary, who art the Virgin made Church [2] and chosen by the most holy Father in heaven, whom He consecrated together with His most holy beloved Son and the Holy Spirit, the Paraclete, [3] in whom there was and is all the fullness of grace and every good. [4] Hail, His palace; hail, His tabernacle; hail, His house. [5] Hail, His raiment; hail, His handmaid; hail, His mother; [6] and hail to you all, holy virtues, who by the grace and enlightenment of the Holy Spirit are poured into the hearts of the faithful, that from unfaithful you may make them faithful to God.
</p>

## A Salutation of the Virtues <a id="salvirt"></a>

<p id="salvirt-1">
[1] Hail, Queen Wisdom, may the Lord keep you with your sister, holy pure Simplicity. [2] Lady holy Poverty, may the Lord keep you with your sister, holy Humility. [3] Lady holy Charity, may the Lord keep you with your sister, holy Obedience. [4] Most holy Virtues, may the Lord keep you all, from whom you come and proceed.
[5] There is not a single soul anywhere in all the world who is able to possess even one of you, unless he first die to himself. [6] Whoever holds one and does not offend the others holds them all. [7] And whoever offends one <ref to="Jas 2:10">holds none and offends them all</ref>. [8] And each one confounds the vices and sins.
[9] Holy Wisdom confounds Satan and all his malice. [10] <ref to="1 Cor 2:6">Pure holy Simplicity confounds all the wisdom of this world</ref> and the wisdom of the flesh. [11] Holy Poverty confounds covetousness and greed and the cares of this present age. [12] Holy Humility confounds pride and all the people who are in the world, and likewise all things that are in the world. [13] <ref to="1 John 4:18">Holy Charity confounds every temptation of the devil and of the flesh, and every carnal fear</ref>. [14] Holy Obedience confounds all the desires of the body and the flesh, [15] and keeps its own body mortified in obedience to the spirit and in obedience to its brother, [16] and is subject and subordinate to all people who are in the world, [17] and not to people alone, but even to all beasts and wild animals, [18] so that they may do with it whatever they please, <ref to="John 19:11">as far as it is granted to them from above by the Lord</ref>.
</p>

## The Testament Made at Siena <a id="testsen"></a>

<p id="testsen-1">
[1] Write down how I bless all my brothers, both those who are now in the Order and those who shall come until the end of the world... [2] Since, by reason of the weakness and suffering of my infirmity, I have not the strength to speak, I lay open to my brothers my will briefly, in these three words, namely: [3] that, as a token of the memory of my blessing and of my testament, they may always love one another; [4] that they may always love and observe our lady holy poverty; [5] and that they may always remain faithful and subject to the prelates and to all the clergy of holy mother Church.
</p>

## The Last Will of Saint Clare <a id="ultvol"></a>

<p id="ultvol-1">
[1] I, Brother Francis, a little one, wish to follow the life and poverty of our most high Lord Jesus Christ and of his most holy Mother, and to persevere in it unto the end; [2] and I beg you, my ladies, and I give you this counsel, that you may always live in this most holy life and poverty. [3] And guard yourselves closely, lest by the teaching or counsel of anyone you should ever in the least depart from it forever.
</p>

## On True and Perfect Joy <a id="vplaet"></a>

<p id="vplaet-1">
[1] The same friar [Leonard] related in that same place that one day the blessed Francis, at Saint Mary's, called Brother Leo and said: Brother Leo, write. [2] He answered: Behold, I am ready. [3] Write, he said, what true joy is. [4] A messenger comes and says that all the masters of Paris have entered the Order: write, this is not true joy. [5] Likewise, that all the prelates beyond the mountains, archbishops and bishops; likewise, that the King of France and the King of England: write, this is not true joy. [6] Likewise, that my brothers have gone to the unbelievers and converted them all to the faith; likewise, that I have so great a grace from God that I heal the sick and work many miracles: I tell you that in all these things there is no true joy. [7] But what is true joy? [8] I am returning from Perugia and in the dead of night I come here, and it is the muddy time of winter, and so cold that icicles of frozen water form at the edges of my tunic and strike constantly against my legs, and blood flows from such wounds. [9] And all covered in mud and cold and ice I come to the gate, and after I have knocked and called for a long time, a brother comes and asks: Who is it? I answer: Brother Francis. [10] And he says: Go away; this is no fitting hour to be abroad; you shall not come in. [11] And when I insist again, let him answer: Go away, you are a simple and ignorant man; you are of no use to us now; we are so many and such men that we have no need of you. [12] And again I stand at the gate and say: For the love of God, take me in this night. [13] And let him answer: I will not. [14] Go to the house of the Crossbearers and ask there. [15] I tell you that if I keep my patience and am not stirred to anger, in this lies true joy and true virtue and the salvation of the soul.
</p>
