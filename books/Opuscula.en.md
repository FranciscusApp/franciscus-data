# MINOR WRITINGS OF SAINT FRANCIS OF ASSISI

## Listen, little poor ones <a id="audite"></a>

<p id="audite-1">
[1] Hear me, little poor ones called by the Lord, you who from many regions and provinces are gathered together: live always in truth, that you may die in obedience. [2] Look not to the outward life, for that of the spirit is the better. I beg you with great love to have discretion in the alms which the Lord gives you. [3] Those who are burdened with infirmity, and the others who wear themselves out in caring for them — bear it all in peace, [4] for you shall sell this labor very dearly, since each of you will be crowned a queen in heaven together with the Virgin Mary.
</p>

## The Blessing Given to Brother Bernard <a id="benbern"></a>

<p id="benbern-1">
[1] Write down what I tell you: [2] The first brother whom the Lord gave me was Brother Bernard, and it was he who first began and most perfectly fulfilled the perfection of the holy Gospel by distributing all his goods to the poor; [3] and on this account, and by reason of many other privileges, I am bound to love him more than any other brother of the whole religious order. [4] Wherefore I will and command, so far as I am able, that whoever shall be minister general love and honor him as he would honor me myself, [5] and that the other provincial ministers and the brothers of the whole order likewise hold him in my stead.
</p>

## The Blessing of Saint Clare <a id="bencl"></a>

<p id="bencl-1">
[1] ...to console her, he wrote to her, sending in his letter [2] his blessing, and moreover he absolved her from every failing, if she had committed any, with respect to his own commands and wishes and the commands and wishes of the Son of God.
</p>

## The blessing given to Brother Leo <a id="benleo"></a>

<p id="benleo-1">
[1] May the Lord bless you and keep you; may he show his face to you and have mercy upon you. [2] <ref to="Num 6:24-26"> May he turn his countenance toward you and grant you peace </ref>. [3] The Lord bless you, Brother Leo, <ref to="Num 6:27">you </ref>.
</p>

## The Canticle of Brother Sun <a id="cantsol"></a>

<p id="cantsol-1">
[1] Most High, all-powerful, good Lord, yours are the praises, the glory, and the honor, and every blessing. [2] To you alone, Most High, do they belong, and no man is worthy to speak your name. [3] Be praised, my Lord, with all your creatures, especially through Sir Brother Sun, who brings the day, and by whom you give us light. [4] And he is beautiful and radiant with great splendor; of you, Most High, he bears the likeness. [5] Be praised, my Lord, through Sister Moon and the stars: in heaven you have formed them clear and precious and fair. [6] Be praised, my Lord, through Brother Wind, and through the air, both cloudy and serene, and every kind of weather, by which you sustain your creatures. [7] Be praised, my Lord, through Sister Water, who is most useful and humble and precious and chaste. [8] Be praised, my Lord, through Brother Fire, by whom you light up the night; and he is beautiful and joyful and mighty and strong. [9] Be praised, my Lord, through our Sister Mother Earth, who sustains and governs us, and brings forth diverse fruits with colored flowers and grass. [10] Be praised, my Lord, through those who grant pardon for love of you, and endure sickness and tribulation. [11] Blessed are those who endure in peace, for by you, Most High, they shall be crowned. [12] Be praised, my Lord, through our Sister Bodily Death, from whom no living man can escape. [13] Woe to those who die in mortal sin; blessed are those whom she will find within your most holy will, for the second death shall do them no harm. [14] Praise and bless my Lord, and give thanks and serve him with great humility.
</p>

## A Letter to Saint Anthony <a id="epant"></a>

<p id="epant-1">
[1] To Brother Anthony, my bishop, Brother Francis sends greeting. [2] It pleases me that you should teach sacred theology to the brothers, provided that amid this study you do not extinguish the spirit of prayer and devotion, as is contained in the Rule.
</p>

## A Letter to the Citizens of Bologna <a id="epbon"></a>

<p id="epbon-1">
He [namely, Brother Martin of Bartona] also related that a certain friar, who was standing in prayer at Brescia on the day of the Lord's Nativity during the earthquake which Saint Francis had foretold and had caused to be proclaimed by the friars throughout all the schools of Bologna, by means of a letter in which there was faulty Latin, was found unharmed beneath the collapse of the stones when the church fell.
</p>

## A Letter of St. Clare concerning Fasting <a id="epcl"></a>

<p id="epcl-1">
[1] Concerning these matters, which you have now bidden me disclose to you—namely, what the feasts were on which our most glorious father Saint Francis specially admonished us to keep a celebration, feasts which, as I suppose, you have somehow imagined lay in a variety of foods—I have judged it fitting to answer your charity: [2] Let your prudence know indeed that, apart from the weak and the sick, for whom he admonished and charged us to make every allowance we could regarding whatever foods, none of us who is healthy and strong should eat anything but Lenten fare only, on ferial days as much as on feast days, fasting every single day except on Sundays and the Nativity of the Lord, on which we were to eat twice in the day. [3] And likewise on Thursdays at the customary seasons it was left to the will of each, so that she who did not wish it was not bound to fast. [4] We who are healthy, however, fast daily except on Sundays and the Nativity. [5] But throughout all of Eastertide, as the writing of blessed Francis says, and on the feasts of holy Mary and of the holy Apostles, we are likewise not bound to fast, unless these feasts should fall on a Friday; [6] and, as has been said before, always, we who are healthy and strong eat Lenten fare.
</p>

## First Letter to the Clergy <a id="epcler1"></a>

<p id="epcler1-1">
[1] Let us all take heed, we who are clerics, of the grave sin and ignorance which some display toward the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and toward his most sacred names and the words written down that sanctify the body. [2] For we know that there can be no body unless it be first sanctified by the word. [3] Indeed we have and behold bodily in this world nothing of the Most High himself save his body and blood, his names and words, <ref to="1 John 3:14">through which we have been made and redeemed from death to life</ref>. [4] Let all those who minister mysteries so holy consider within themselves — above all those who minister unworthily — how mean are the chalices, the corporals, and the linens where the body and blood of that same Lord are made a sacrifice. [5] And in many places it is set down and left in unworthy spots, carried about wretchedly, received unworthily, and dispensed to others without discernment. [6] His names too and his written words are at times trampled underfoot; [7] <ref to="1 Cor 2:14">for the animal man does not perceive the things that are of God</ref>. [8] Are we not moved by devotion at all this, when the merciful Lord himself gives himself into our hands, and we handle him and daily take him into our mouths? [9] Or are we ignorant that we must come into his hands? [10] Therefore let us swiftly and firmly amend all these things and others besides; [11] and wherever the most holy body of our Lord Jesus Christ has been unlawfully placed and abandoned, let it be removed from that place and set and enshrined in a precious spot. [12] Likewise the names and written words of the Lord, wherever they are found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and set in a fitting place. [13] To all these things, unto the end of the world, all clerics are bound to attend above all else. [14] And those who do not do so <ref to="Matt 12:36">let them know that they must render account before our Lord Jesus Christ on the day of judgment</ref>. [15] And that this writing may be the better observed, let those who make copies of it know that they are blessed by the Lord God.
</p>

## Second Letter to the Clergy <a id="epcler2"></a>

<p id="epcler2-1">
[1] Let us take heed, all we clerics, of the grievous sin and the ignorance that certain men display toward the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and toward his most sacred names and written words, which consecrate the body. [2] For we know that there can be no body unless it is first consecrated by the word. [3] Indeed, in this world we possess and behold bodily nothing of the Most High himself except the body and blood, the names and words, <ref to="1 John 3:14">through which we were made and redeemed from death unto life</ref>. [4] Let all those who administer such most holy mysteries consider within themselves—above all those who administer them without discernment—how mean are the chalices, the corporals, and the linens upon which the body and blood of our Lord is sacrificed. [5] And by many it is left in unworthy places, carried about miserably, received unworthily, and dispensed to others without discernment. [6] His written names and words are at times trampled underfoot; [7] <ref to="1 Cor 2:14">for the natural man does not perceive the things that are of God</ref>. [8] Are we not stirred to devotion by all these things, when the merciful Lord himself gives himself into our hands, and we handle him and daily receive him upon our lips? [9] Or are we ignorant that we must come into his hands? [10] Therefore let us swiftly and firmly amend ourselves in all these matters and others besides; [11] and wherever the most holy body of our Lord Jesus Christ may have been unlawfully placed and abandoned, let it be removed from that place and set and secured in a precious place. [12] Likewise the written names and words of the Lord, wherever they are found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and be placed in a fitting spot. [13] And we know that we are bound to observe all these things above all else, according to the commands of the Lord and the constitutions of holy mother Church. [14] And whoever fails to do this, let him know that <ref to="Matt 12:36">he shall render an account before our Lord Jesus Christ on the day of judgment</ref>. [15] And that this writing may be the better observed, let those who make copies of it know that they are blessed by the Lord God.
</p>

## First Letter to the Custodians <a id="epcust1"></a>

<p id="epcust1-1">
[1] To all the custodians of the Friars Minor to whom these letters shall come, Brother Francis, your servant and little one in the Lord God, sends greeting together with new signs of heaven and earth, which in God's sight are great and most excellent, yet by many religious and by other men are reckoned as of the least account.
[2] I beseech you, more than on my own behalf, that when it is fitting and you see it to be expedient, you humbly entreat the clergy that above all things they ought to venerate the most holy body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, and his holy names and written words, which sanctify the body. [3] They ought to hold as precious the chalices, the corporals, the ornaments of the altar, and all things that pertain to the sacrifice. [4] And if in any place the most holy body of the Lord shall have been set in a very poor manner, let it be placed and enshrined by them in a precious spot according to the command of the Church, and let it be carried with great veneration and administered to others with discretion. [5] The names too and the written words of the Lord, wherever they may be found in unclean places, let them be gathered up and set in an honorable place. [6] And in every sermon that you preach, admonish the people concerning penance, and that no one can be saved <ref to="John 6:54">unless he receive the most holy body and blood of the Lord</ref>. [7] And when it is offered in sacrifice by the priest upon the altar and is borne to any place, let all peoples upon bended knees render praise, glory, and honor to the Lord God, the living and the true. [8] And of his praise proclaim and preach thus to all peoples, that at every hour, and whenever the bells are rung, praises and thanks may ever be rendered by the whole people to the almighty God throughout all the earth.
[9] And to whatever of my brother custodians this writing shall come, and who shall copy it and keep it by them, and who shall have it copied for the brothers who hold the office of preaching and the custody of the brothers, and who shall preach all the things contained in this writing unto the end—let them know that they have the blessing of the Lord God and my own. [10] And let these things be for them by true and holy obedience. Amen.
</p>

## Second Letter to the Custodians <a id="epcust2"></a>

<p id="epcust2-1">
[1] To all the custodians of the Friars Minor to whom these letters shall come, Brother Francis, least of the servants of God, sends greeting and holy peace in the Lord.
[2] Know that in the sight of God there are certain matters exceedingly lofty and sublime, which are sometimes reckoned among men as base and worthless; [3] and there are others precious and esteemed among men which before God are held as most vile and contemptible. [4] I beg you before the Lord our God, as earnestly as I am able, that those letters which treat of the most holy Body and Blood of our Lord you hand over to the bishops and to other clerics; [5] and keep in mind the charges we have laid upon you concerning these things. [6] As for the other letters which I send you, that you may hand them to the magistrates, consuls, and rulers, and in which it is contained that God's praises be proclaimed publicly among the peoples and in the squares, make at once many copies, [7] and with great diligence deliver them to those to whom they ought to be given.
</p>

## First Letter to the Faithful <a id="epfid1"></a>

<aside>
In the name of the Lord!
</aside>

<p id="epfid1-1" label="Of those who do penance" label_format="heading">
[1] All who love the Lord with their whole heart, their whole soul and mind, <ref to="Mark 12:30">with their whole strength,</ref> <ref to="Matt 22:39">and love their neighbors as themselves,</ref> [2] and hold their bodies in hatred together with their vices and sins, [3] and receive the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, [4] and bring forth fruits worthy of penance: [5] Oh how blessed and blessed indeed are those men and women, so long as they do such things and persevere in such things, [6] <ref to="Isa 11:2">for the spirit of the Lord shall rest upon them</ref> <ref to="John 14:23">and shall make among them a dwelling place and abode,</ref> [7] <ref to="Matt 5:45">and they are the children of the heavenly Father,</ref> whose works they do, and they are the spouses, <ref to="Matt 12:50">the brothers and mothers of our Lord Jesus Christ.</ref> [8] We are spouses when the faithful soul is joined by the Holy Spirit to our Lord Jesus Christ. [9] We are His brothers <ref to="Matt 12:50">when we do the will of the Father who is in heaven;</ref> [10] we are mothers <ref to="1 Cor 6:20">when we bear Him in our heart and body</ref> through divine love and a pure and sincere conscience; we give Him birth through holy work, <ref to="Matt 5:16">which ought to shine before others as an example.</ref> [11] Oh how glorious it is, how holy and great, to have a Father in heaven! [12] Oh how holy it is to have such a Spouse—the Comforter, beautiful and wonderful! [13] Oh how holy and how beloved, how pleasing, humble, peaceable, sweet, lovable, and above all things desirable it is to have such a Brother and such a Son: our Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="John 10:15">who laid down His life for His sheep</ref> and prayed to the Father, saying: [14] Holy Father, <ref to="John 17:11">keep in Thy name those</ref> whom Thou hast given me in the world; <ref to="John 17:6">they were Thine and Thou gavest them to me.</ref> [15] And the words which Thou gavest me I have given to them, and they have received and truly believed that I came forth from Thee, and they have known <ref to="John 17:8">that Thou hast sent me.</ref> [16] <ref to="John 17:9">I pray for them and not for the world.</ref> [17] <ref to="John 17:17">Bless and sanctify them,</ref> <ref to="John 17:19">and for them I sanctify myself.</ref> [18] I pray not for them only, <ref to="John 17:20">but also for those who through their word shall believe in me,</ref> <ref to="John 17:23">that they may be sanctified into one</ref> <ref to="John 17:11">even as we are.</ref> [19] And I will, Father, that where I am, they too may be with me, <ref to="John 17:24">that they may behold my glory</ref> <ref to="Matt 20:21">in Thy kingdom.</ref> Amen.
</p>

<p id="epfid1-2" label="Concerning those who do not do penance" label_format="heading">
[1] But all those men and women who do not live in penance, [2] and do not receive the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, [3] and who practice vice and sin, and who walk after the evil concupiscence and the evil desires of their flesh, [4] and do not keep what they have promised to the Lord, [5] and serve the world in a bodily way through the desires of the flesh and the anxieties of the age and the cares of this life: [6] Such are held fast by the devil, <ref to="John 8:41">whose children they are and whose works they do</ref>, [7] and they are blind, for they do not behold the true light, our Lord Jesus Christ. [8] They possess no spiritual wisdom, since they do not have the Son of God, who is the true wisdom of the Father; [9] of them it is said: <ref to="Ps 106:27">Their wisdom was swallowed up</ref>, and: <ref to="Ps 118:21">Cursed are they who turn aside from thy commandments</ref>. [10] They see and recognize, they know and do what is evil, and knowingly they destroy their own souls. [11] Behold, you blind ones, deceived by your enemies—by the flesh, the world, and the devil—for to the body it is sweet to commit sin and bitter to serve God; [12] since all vices and sins come forth and proceed from the heart of men, <ref to="Mark 7:21">as the Lord says in the Gospel</ref>. [13] And you have nothing in this age nor in the age to come. [14] You imagine that you will long possess the vanities of this world, but you are deceived, for a day and an hour will come of which you do not think, which you do not know and do not foresee; the body grows sick, death draws near, and thus one dies a bitter death. [15] And wherever, whenever, and however a man dies in mortal sin without penance and satisfaction—if he can make satisfaction and does not—the devil snatches his soul from his body with such anguish and tribulation as no one can know save the one who suffers it. [16] <ref to="2 Chr 1:12">And all the talents and power and knowledge and wisdom</ref> which they thought they possessed <ref to="Luke 8:18; Mark 4:25">shall be taken away from them</ref>. [17] They leave all to their kinsmen and friends, and these have carried off and divided his substance, and afterward have said: Cursed be his soul, for he could have given us more and gained more than he did gain. [18] The worms devour his body, and so they have lost both body and soul in this brief age, and they shall go into hell, where they shall be tormented without end.
[19] All those to whom this letter shall come we entreat <ref to="1 John 4:16">in the charity which is God</ref>, that they may graciously receive with divine love these fragrant words of our Lord Jesus Christ set forth above. [20] And let those who cannot read have them read to them often; [21] and let them keep them by them, with holy conduct, unto the end, <ref to="John 6:64">for they are spirit and life</ref>. [22] And those who do not do this <ref to="Matt 12:36">shall be bound to render an account on the day of judgment</ref> <ref to="Rom 14:10">before the tribunal of our Lord Jesus Christ</ref>.
</p>

## Second Letter to the Faithful <a id="epfid2"></a>

<p id="epfid2-1">
In the name of the Lord, the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit. Amen.
[1] To all Christian religious, clerics and laypeople, men and women, to all who dwell throughout the whole world, brother Francis, their servant and subject, offers homage with reverence, true peace from heaven and sincere charity in the Lord.
[2] Since I am the servant of all, I am bound to serve all and to dispense to them the fragrant words of my Lord. [3] Wherefore, pondering in my mind that, since because of the sickness and weakness of my body I cannot visit each one in person, I have resolved by this present letter and by messengers to convey to you the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, who is the Word of the Father, and the words of the Holy Spirit, <ref to="John 6:64">which are spirit and life</ref>.
[4] This Word of the Father, so worthy, so holy and glorious, the most high Father proclaimed from heaven through his holy angel Gabriel into the womb of the holy and glorious Virgin Mary, from whose womb he received the true flesh of our humanity and frailty. [5] He who, <ref to="2 Cor 8:9">though he was rich</ref> above all things, willed himself to choose poverty in the world together with the most blessed Virgin, his mother. [6] And near his passion he celebrated the Passover with his disciples, and taking bread he gave thanks and blessed and broke it, saying: Take and eat, <ref to="Matt 26:26">this is my body</ref>. [7] And taking the cup he said: This is my blood of the new testament, <ref to="Matt 26:27">which shall be poured out for you and for many unto the remission of sins</ref>. [8] Then he prayed to the Father, saying: Father, if it be possible, <ref to="Matt 26:39">let this cup pass from me</ref>. [9] <ref to="Luke 22:44">And his sweat became as drops of blood running down upon the ground</ref>. [10] Yet he placed his own will within the will of the Father, saying: Father, <ref to="Matt 26:42">thy will be done</ref>; not as I will, <ref to="Matt 26:39">but as thou wilt</ref>. [11] Such was the will of that Father, that his blessed and glorious Son, whom he gave to us and who was born for us, should offer himself through his own blood as a sacrifice and victim upon the altar of the cross; [12] not for himself, <ref to="John 1:3">through whom all things were made</ref>, but for our sins, [13] leaving us an example, <ref to="1 Pet 2:21">that we should follow in his footsteps</ref>. [14] And he wills that all of us be saved through him and that we receive him with a pure heart and a chaste body. [15] But few there are who are willing to receive him and to be saved through him, <ref to="Matt 11:30">although his yoke is sweet and his burden light</ref>.
[16] Those who refuse to taste <ref to="Ps 33:9">how sweet the Lord is</ref><ref to="John 3:19">and love darkness rather than light</ref>, unwilling to fulfill the commandments of God, are accursed; [17] of whom it is said through the prophet: <ref to="Ps 118:21">Cursed are they who turn aside from thy commandments</ref>. [18] But, oh how blessed and happy are those who love God and act as the Lord himself says in the gospel: Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with thy whole heart and with thy whole mind, <ref to="Matt 22:37">and thy neighbor as thyself</ref>.
[19] Let us therefore love God and adore him with a pure heart and a pure mind, for he, seeking this above all things, said: <ref to="John 4:23">True worshippers shall adore the Father in spirit and in truth</ref>. [20] For all who adore him <ref to="John 4:24">must adore him in the spirit of truth</ref>. [21] <ref to="Ps 31:4">And let us render to him praises and prayers day and night</ref>, saying: Our Father, <ref to="Matt 6:9">who art in heaven</ref>, <ref to="Luke 18:1">for we ought always to pray and not to faint</ref>.
[22] We ought indeed to confess all our sins to a priest; and let us receive from him the body and blood of our Lord Jesus Christ. [23] <ref to="John 6:55">He who does not eat his flesh and does not drink his blood</ref> <ref to="John 3:5">cannot enter into the kingdom of God</ref>. [24] Yet let him eat and drink worthily, for whoever receives unworthily eats and drinks judgment to himself, <ref to="1 Cor 11:29">not discerning the body of the Lord</ref>, that is, not distinguishing it. [25] <ref to="Luke 3:8">Let us moreover bring forth fruits worthy of penance</ref>. [26] <ref to="Matt 22:39">And let us love our neighbors as ourselves</ref>. [27] And if anyone is unwilling to love them as himself, let him at least not do them harm, but rather do them good.
[28] But those who have received the power to judge others, let them exercise judgment with mercy, just as they themselves desire to obtain mercy from the Lord. [29] <ref to="Jas 2:13">For judgment without mercy shall be to those who have not shown mercy</ref>. [30] Let us therefore have charity and humility; and let us give alms, <ref to="Tob 4:11; Tob 12:9">for these wash souls from the filth of sins</ref>. [31] For men lose all the things that they leave behind in this world; yet they carry with them the reward of charity and the alms they have given, for which they will have from the Lord a prize and a worthy recompense.
[32] <ref to="Sir 3:32">We ought also to fast and abstain from vices and sins</ref> and from excess of food and drink, and to be Catholic. [33] We ought also to visit churches frequently and to venerate and reverence clerics, not so much on their own account, if they be sinners, but for the office and the administration of the most holy body and blood of Christ, which they sacrifice upon the altar and receive and administer to others. [34] And let us all firmly know that no one can be saved except through the holy words and the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, which the clerics speak, announce, and minister. [35] And they alone ought to minister these, and no others. [36] But especially the religious, who have renounced the world, are bound to do more and greater things, <ref to="Luke 11:42">yet without omitting these</ref>.
[37] We ought to hold our bodies in hatred with their vices and sins, for the Lord says in the gospel: All evils, <ref to="Matt 15:18-19; Mark 7:23">vices and sins proceed from the heart</ref>. [38] We ought to love our enemies and to do good to those <ref to="Matt 5:44; Luke 6:27">who hold us in hatred</ref>. [39] We ought to observe the precepts and counsels of our Lord Jesus Christ. [40] <ref to="Matt 16:24">We ought also to deny ourselves</ref> and to place our bodies beneath the yoke of servitude and holy obedience, just as each one has promised the Lord. [41] And let no one be bound out of obedience to obey another in that wherein an offense or sin is committed.
[42] <ref to="Luke 22:26">But he to whom obedience has been entrusted, and who is held to be greater, let him be as the lesser</ref> and the servant of the other brothers. [43] And toward each of his brothers let him show and have such mercy as he would wish to be shown to himself, <ref to="Matt 7:12">were he in the like case</ref>. [44] Nor let him grow angry at a brother because of his fault, but with all patience and humility let him kindly admonish and bear with him.
[45] We ought not to be wise and prudent according to the flesh, but rather we ought to be simple, humble, and pure. [46] And let us hold our bodies in reproach and contempt, for through our own fault we are all wretched and rotten, foul, and worms, as the Lord says through the prophet: I am a worm and no man, <ref to="Ps 21:7">the reproach of men and the outcast of the people</ref>. [47] Never ought we to desire to be above others, <ref to="1 Pet 2:13">but rather we ought to be servants and subject to every human creature for God's sake</ref>. [48] And all those men and women, when they have done such things and persevered unto the end, <ref to="Isa 11:2">the Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon them</ref><ref to="John 14:23">and shall make within them his dwelling and abode</ref>. [49] <ref to="Matt 5:45">And they shall be children of the heavenly Father</ref>, whose works they do. [50] And they are spouses, <ref to="Matt 12:50">brothers and mothers of our Lord Jesus Christ</ref>. [51] We are spouses when the faithful soul is joined by the Holy Spirit to Jesus Christ. [52] We are brothers, indeed, when we do the will of his Father, <ref to="Matt 12:50">who is in heaven</ref>; [53] mothers, <ref to="1 Cor 6:20">when we bear him in our heart and body</ref> through love and a pure and sincere conscience; we give him birth through holy action, <ref to="Matt 5:16">which ought to shine before others as an example</ref>.
[54] O how glorious and holy and great it is to have a Father in heaven! [55] O how holy it is to have a comforter, beautiful and wonderful, as a spouse! [56] O how holy and how beloved, well-pleasing, humble, peaceful, sweet and lovable and above all things desirable it is to have such a brother and son, <ref to="John 10:15">who laid down his life for his sheep</ref> and prayed to the Father for us, saying: Holy Father, keep in thy name those <ref to="John 17:11">whom thou hast given me</ref>. [57] Father, all whom thou hast given me in the world <ref to="John 17:6">were thine, and thou gavest them to me</ref>. [58] And the words which thou gavest me I gave to them; and they received them and truly knew that I came forth from thee, and they believed <ref to="John 17:8">that thou didst send me</ref>; <ref to="John 17:9">I pray for them and not for the world</ref>; <ref to="John 17:17">bless and sanctify them</ref>. [59] And for them I sanctify myself, <ref to="John 17:19">that they may be sanctified into</ref><ref to="John 17:11">one, as we</ref> are. [60] And I will, Father, that where I am, there they too may be with me, <ref to="John 17:24">that they may behold my glory</ref><ref to="Matt 20:21">in thy kingdom</ref>.

## Exposition on the Our Father <a id="exppat"></a>

<p id="exppat-1">
[1] O most holy Father: our creator, redeemer, comforter, and savior.
[2] Who art in heaven: in the angels and in the saints; illuminating them unto knowledge, for thou, Lord, art light; enkindling them unto love, for thou, Lord, art love; dwelling within them and filling them unto blessedness, for thou, Lord, art the supreme good, the eternal good, from whom comes every good, without whom there is no good.
[3] Hallowed be thy name: may the knowledge of thee grow bright in us, that we may know <ref to="Eph 3:18">what is the breadth </ref> of thy benefits, the length of thy promises, the height of thy majesty, and the depth of thy judgments.
[4] Thy kingdom come: that thou mayest reign in us through grace and cause us to come to thy kingdom, where there is the open vision of thee, the perfect love of thee, the blessed fellowship of thee, and the everlasting enjoyment of thee.
[5] Thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven: <ref to="Luke 10:27">that we may love thee with our whole heart </ref> by ever thinking of thee, with our whole soul by ever longing for thee, with our whole mind by directing all our intentions toward thee, seeking thy honor in all things, and by spending with all our strength all our powers and the senses of soul and body in the service of thy love and in nothing else; and that we may love our neighbors as ourselves, drawing all men as far as we are able to thy love, <ref to="2 Cor 6:3">rejoicing in the good of others as in our own, suffering with them in their misfortunes, and giving offense to no one </ref>.
[6] Our daily bread: thy beloved Son, our Lord Jesus Christ, give us this day: in memory and understanding and reverence of the love which he bore toward us, and of those things which for us he said, did, and endured.
[7] And forgive us our debts: through thy ineffable mercy, through the power of the passion of thy beloved Son, and through the merits and intercession of the most blessed Virgin and of all thine elect.
[8] As we also forgive our debtors: and what we do not fully forgive, do thou, Lord, make us fully forgive, that for thy sake we may truly love our enemies and devoutly intercede for them before thee, <ref to="1 Thess 5:15">rendering to no one evil for evil </ref>, and striving in all things to be of profit in thee.
[9] And lead us not into temptation: whether hidden or manifest, sudden or persistent.
[10] But deliver us from evil: past, present, and to come. Glory be to the Father, etc.
</p>

## Fragments of another Rule <a id="fragm"></a>

<p id="fragm-1" label="I. Fragments of the Worcester Codex (Codex Wo)" label_format="heading">
[1] Let us all take heed, brothers, to what the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:44">Love your enemies and do good to those who hate you </ref>, for our Lord Jesus Christ, <ref to="1 Pet 2:21">whose footsteps we ought to follow </ref>, called his own betrayer a friend, and of his own accord offered himself to those who crucified him. [2] Therefore our friends are all those who unjustly inflict upon us tribulations, distresses, shame, injuries, sorrows and torments, martyrdom and death; and them we ought to love greatly, for through what they inflict upon us we gain eternal life. [3] And let us chastise our body, crucifying it together with its vices, its lusts, and its sins, for by living according to the flesh it seeks to strip us of the love of Jesus Christ and of eternal life, and to cast itself, along with the soul, into hell; [4] for by our own fault we were foul, opposed to the good, and prompt and eager toward evil; [5] for as the Lord says: <ref to="Mark 7:21">«From the heart proceed and come forth evil thoughts» </ref> and so on...
[6] But now, since we have forsaken the world, we have nothing else to do but to be diligent in following his will and in pleasing him alone; [7] let us take great care lest we be the ground beside the way, or stony or thorny, according to what the Lord says in the Gospel: <ref to="Luke 8:11">«The seed is the word of God </ref>; [8] but that which fell beside the way, <ref to="Luke 8:5">was trodden underfoot» </ref> and so on up to where it says: <ref to="Luke 8:15">«they bring forth fruit in patience» </ref>.
[9] And therefore, all you brothers, as the Lord says: «Let us leave the dead... <ref to="Matt 8:22">to bury their own» </ref>; [10] and let us take great care against the malice and cunning of Satan, who wills that a man should not have his strength and his heart turned toward the Lord God; [11] and, prowling about, he longs, under the guise of some reward or assistance, to seize the heart of man and to smother the word and commandments of the Lord out of his memory, wishing to inhabit and blind the heart of man through worldly affairs and cares, as the Lord says: [12] <ref to="Matt 12:43">«When the unclean spirit» </ref> and so on up to where it says: [13] <ref to="Matt 12:45">«The last state of that man becomes worse than the first» </ref>.
[14] Wherefore, all you brothers, let us guard ourselves greatly, lest under the guise of some work or reward or assistance we lose or turn away our mind and heart from the Lord. [15] But in charity, <ref to="1 John 4:16">which is God </ref>, I beseech all the brothers, ministers as well as the rest, that with every hindrance removed and every care and anxiety set aside, wherever they can, they may love, serve, and adore the Lord God with a clean heart and a pure mind—for this he seeks above all else. ## A Rule for Hermitages <a id="reger"></a>

<p id="reger-1">
[1] Those who wish to live a life of religion in hermitages should be three brothers, or at most four; let two of them be mothers and have two sons, or at least one. [2] Let these two who are mothers <ref to="Luke 10:38-42">keep to the life of Martha, and let the two sons keep to the life of Mary</ref>, and let them have one enclosure in which each may have his own little cell, where he may pray and sleep. [3] And let them always say Compline of the day immediately after sunset; and let them be zealous to keep silence; and let them say their hours; <ref to="Matt 6:33">and at Matins let them rise and first of all seek the kingdom of God and his righteousness</ref>. [4] And let them say Prime at the hour that is fitting, and after Terce let them break silence; and they may speak and go to their mothers. [5] And, when it pleases them, they may ask alms of them like little poor children, for the love of the Lord God. [6] And afterward let them say Sext and None; and let them say Vespers at the fitting hour. [7] And in the enclosure where they dwell let them not allow any person to enter, nor let them eat there. [8] Let those brothers who are mothers strive to remain apart from every person; and through obedience to their minister let them guard their sons from every person, so that no one may speak with them. [9] And let these sons not speak with any person save with their mothers and with their minister and their custodian, whenever it may please these to visit them with the blessing of the Lord God. [10] But the sons shall at times take up the office of the mothers, according as it shall seem fitting to arrange in turn for a season; and let them strive carefully and zealously to observe all the things said above.
</p>

[12] Holy Humility confounds pride and all the people who are in the world, and likewise all things that are in the world. [13] <ref to="1 John 4:18">Holy Charity confounds every temptation of the devil and of the flesh, and every carnal fear</ref>. [14] Holy Obedience confounds all the desires of the body and the flesh, [15] and keeps its own body mortified in obedience to the spirit and in obedience to its brother, [16] and is subject and subordinate to all people who are in the world, [17] and not to people alone, but even to all beasts and wild animals, [18] so that they may do with it whatever they please, <ref to="John 19:11">as far as it is granted to them from above by the Lord</ref>.
</p>

## The Testament Made at Siena <a id="testsen"></a>

<p id="testsen-1">
[1] Write down how I bless all my brothers, both those who are now in the Order and those who shall come until the end of the world... [2] Since, by reason of the weakness and suffering of my infirmity, I have not the strength to speak, I lay open to my brothers my will briefly, in these three words, namely: [3] that, as a token of the memory of my blessing and of my testament, they may always love one another; [4] that they may always love and observe our lady holy poverty; [5] and that they may always remain faithful and subject to the prelates and to all the clergy of holy mother Church.
</p>

## The Last Will of Saint Clare <a id="ultvol"></a>

<p id="ultvol-1">
[1] I, Brother Francis, a little one, wish to follow the life and poverty of our most high Lord Jesus Christ and of his most holy Mother, and to persevere in it unto the end; [2] and I beg you, my ladies, and I give you this counsel, that you may always live in this most holy life and poverty. [3] And guard yourselves closely, lest by the teaching or counsel of anyone you should ever in the least depart from it forever.
</p>

## On True and Perfect Joy <a id="vplaet"></a>

<p id="vplaet-1">
[1] The same friar [Leonard] related in that same place that one day the blessed Francis, at Saint Mary's, called Brother Leo and said: Brother Leo, write. [2] He answered: Behold, I am ready. [3] Write, he said, what true joy is. [4] A messenger comes and says that all the masters of Paris have entered the Order: write, this is not true joy. [5] Likewise, that all the prelates beyond the mountains, archbishops and bishops; likewise, that the King of France and the King of England: write, this is not true joy. [6] Likewise, that my brothers have gone to the unbelievers and converted them all to the faith; likewise, that I have so great a grace from God that I heal the sick and work many miracles: I tell you that in all these things there is no true joy. [7] But what is true joy? [8] I am returning from Perugia and in the dead of night I come here, and it is the muddy time of winter, and so cold that icicles of frozen water form at the edges of my tunic and strike constantly against my legs, and blood flows from such wounds. [9] And all covered in mud and cold and ice I come to the gate, and after I have knocked and called for a long time, a brother comes and asks: Who is it? I answer: Brother Francis. [10] And he says: Go away; this is no fitting hour to be abroad; you shall not come in. [11] And when I insist again, let him answer: Go away, you are a simple and ignorant man; you are of no use to us now; we are so many and such men that we have no need of you. [12] And again I stand at the gate and say: For the love of God, take me in this night. [13] And let him answer: I will not. [14] Go to the house of the Crossbearers and ask there. [15] I tell you that if I keep my patience and am not stirred to anger, in this lies true joy and true virtue and the salvation of the soul.
</p>
