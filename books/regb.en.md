# The Later Rule

## I. In the name of the Lord! Here begins the life of the Friars Minor <a id="1"></a>

<p id="1-1">
[1] This is the Rule and Life of the Friars Minor: namely, to observe the holy Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ by living in obedience, without anything of one's own, and in chastity. [2] Brother Francis promises obedience and reverence to the lord Pope Honorius and to his successors canonically elected, and to the Roman Church. [3] And let the other brothers be bound to obey Brother Francis and his successors.
</p>

## II. Concerning those who wish to embrace this way of life, and how they ought to be received. <a id="2"></a>

<p id="2-1">
[1] If any wish to embrace this life and come to our brothers, let them be sent to their provincial ministers, to whom alone, and to no others, the permission to receive brothers is granted. [2] Let the ministers examine them diligently concerning the catholic faith and the sacraments of the Church. [3] And if they believe all these things and are willing faithfully to confess them and to observe them steadfastly to the end, [4] and if they have no wives, or, if they have, and their wives have already entered a monastery, or have granted them leave by the authority of the diocesan bishop, a vow of continence having already been made, and their wives are of such an age that no suspicion can arise concerning them, [5] <ref to="Matt 19:21"> let them speak to them the word of the holy Gospel </ref>, that they should go and sell all that they have and take care to distribute it to the poor. [6] But if they are unable to do this, their good will suffices. [7] And let the brothers and their ministers take heed not to be anxious about their temporal goods, so that these may freely dispose of their own possessions in whatever way the Lord shall inspire them. [8] If, however, counsel is sought, the ministers may have leave to send them to certain God-fearing persons, by whose advice their goods may be bestowed upon the poor. [9] Afterward let them grant them the garments of probation, namely two tunics without a hood, and a cord, and breeches, and a caperon reaching to the cord, [10] unless it should at some time seem otherwise to those same ministers according to God. [11] But when the year of probation is completed, let them be received into obedience, promising always to observe this life and rule. [12] And in no way shall it be permitted them to leave this religion, according to the command of the lord Pope, [13] <ref to="Luke 9:62"> because, according to the holy Gospel, no one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God </ref>. [14] And those who have already promised obedience may have one tunic with a hood and, if they wish to have it, another without a hood. [15] And those compelled by necessity may wear shoes. [16] And let all the brothers be clothed in poor garments, and they may patch them with sackcloth and other pieces, with the blessing of God. [17] Whom I admonish and exhort not to despise or judge those men whom they see clothed in soft and colored garments, using delicate food and drink, but rather let each one judge and despise himself.
</p>

## III. Concerning the Divine Office and fasting, and how the brothers ought to go through the world. <a id="3"></a>

<p id="3-1">
[1] Let the clerics perform the divine office according to the rite of the holy Roman Church, save for the psalter, [2] for which they may possess breviaries. [3] The lay brothers, however, are to recite twenty-four Our Fathers for matins, five for lauds, and for prime, terce, sext, and none, seven for each of these; for vespers twelve, and for compline seven; [4] and let them pray for the dead. [5] And let them fast from the feast of All Saints until the Nativity of the Lord. [6] But as for the holy Lent that begins at the Epiphany and runs forty continuous days, <ref to="Matt 4:2">which the Lord consecrated by his own holy fast</ref>, those who fast it willingly shall be blessed by the Lord, while those who choose not to are under no obligation. [7] Yet the other Lent, up to the Lord's Resurrection, let them fast. [8] At other times they need not fast except on Fridays. [9] But in a time of manifest necessity the brothers are not bound to bodily fasting. [10] I counsel, admonish, and exhort my brothers in the Lord Jesus Christ that, when they go about the world, <ref to="2 Tim 2:14">they neither quarrel nor contend in words</ref>, nor pass judgment on others; [11] but rather let them be gentle, peaceable, and modest, meek and humble, speaking honorably to all, as is fitting. [12] And they ought not to ride on horseback unless compelled by manifest necessity or infirmity. [13] Into whatever house they enter, let them first say: <ref to="Luke 10:5">Peace to this house</ref>. [14] And according to the holy Gospel, of all the foods that are set before them, <ref to="Luke 10:8">let it be lawful to eat</ref>.
</p>

## IV. That the brothers should not receive money. <a id="4"></a>

<p id="4-1">
[1] I firmly command all the brothers that in no way are they to accept coins or money, whether by themselves or through some intermediary. [2] Nevertheless, for the needs of the sick and for clothing the other brothers, let only the ministers and custodians exercise a diligent care through spiritual friends, according to the places and seasons and cold regions, as they shall see to be expedient for necessity; [3] this always being preserved, that, as has been said, they are not to accept coins or money.
</p>

## V. On the manner of working. <a id="5"></a>

<p id="5-1">
[1] Let those brothers to whom the Lord has granted the grace of working labor faithfully and devoutly, [2] in such a way that, banishing the idleness that is the soul's enemy, they do not extinguish the spirit of holy prayer and devotion, to which all other temporal things ought to be subservient. [3] As payment for their labor, let them receive, for themselves and their brothers, the necessities of the body, but not coins or money, [4] and this humbly, as befits the servants of God and the followers of most holy poverty.
</p>

## VI. That the brothers should appropriate nothing to themselves, and concerning the seeking of alms and concerning the sick brothers. <a id="6"></a>

<p id="6-1">
[1] Let the brothers claim nothing for themselves, neither house, nor place, nor anything at all. [2] And <ref to="1 Pet 2:11">as pilgrims and strangers</ref> in this world, serving the Lord in poverty and humility, let them go confidently to seek alms, [3] nor need they be ashamed, <ref to="2 Cor 8:9">for the Lord made himself poor for us in this world</ref>. [4] This is that lofty height of most exalted poverty, which has made you, my dearest brothers, heirs and kings of the kingdom of heaven, has made you poor in possessions and <ref to="Jas 2:5">exalted you in virtues</ref>. [5] Let this be your portion, <ref to="Ps 141:6">which leads into the land of the living</ref>. [6] Cleaving wholly to it, my most beloved brothers, may you desire to have nothing else forever under heaven for the name of our Lord Jesus Christ. [7] And wherever the brothers are and find themselves, let them show themselves as members of one household among one another. [8] And let each one confidently make known his need to another, for <ref to="1 Thess 2:7">if a mother nourishes and loves her child</ref> according to the flesh, how much more diligently ought one to love and nourish his spiritual brother? [9] And if any of them falls into sickness, the other brothers ought to serve him, <ref to="Matt 7:12">just as they would wish to be served themselves</ref>.
</p>

## VII. On the penance to be imposed upon brothers who sin. <a id="7"></a>

<p id="7-1">
[1] If any of the friars, at the enemy's prompting, should sin mortally, then—concerning those sins about which it has been decreed among the friars that recourse be had to the provincial ministers alone—these friars are bound to have recourse to them as swiftly as they can, without delay. [2] And the ministers themselves, if they are priests, shall with mercy enjoin a penance upon them; but if they are not priests, they shall have it enjoined through other priests of the order, as shall seem to them, according to God, most fittingly expedient. [3] And they must take care not to grow angry or troubled on account of anyone's sin, for anger and disturbance impede charity both in themselves and in others.
</p>

## VIII. Concerning the election of the general minister of this brotherhood, and concerning the Pentecost chapter. <a id="8"></a>

<p id="8-1">
[1] Let all the brothers be bound always to have as general minister and servant of the whole brotherhood one of the brothers of this religion, and let them be bound to obey him firmly. [2] When he dies, let the election of his successor be made by the provincial ministers and the custodians in the chapter of Pentecost, at which the provincial ministers shall always be bound to assemble together, wherever it shall have been appointed by the general minister; [3] and this once every three years, or at some other interval, greater or lesser, according as it shall have been ordained by the aforesaid minister. [4] And if at any time it should appear to the whole body of provincial ministers and custodians that the aforesaid minister is not adequate for the service and common good of the brothers, the aforesaid brothers, to whom the election is entrusted, shall be bound to elect another for themselves as custodian in the name of the Lord. [5] But after the chapter of Pentecost the ministers and custodians may each, if they wish and it shall seem expedient to them, in that same year summon their brothers once to a chapter in their custodies.
</p>

## IX. Concerning the Preachers. <a id="9"></a>

<p id="9-1">
[1] Let the brothers not preach in the diocese of any bishop when they have been forbidden to do so by him. [2] And let no brother whatsoever dare to preach to the people unless he has been examined and approved by the minister general of this fraternity, and the office of preaching has been granted to him by that same minister. [3] I also admonish and exhort these same brothers that, in the preaching they perform, <ref to="Ps 11:7; Ps 17:31">their words be examined and chaste </ref>, for the benefit and edification of the people, [4] proclaiming to them vices and virtues, punishment and glory with brevity of speech; <ref to="Rom 9:28">for the Lord made a shortened word upon the earth </ref>.
</p>

## X. On the admonition and correction of the brothers. <a id="10"></a>

<p id="10-1">
[1] Those brothers who are ministers and servants of the other brothers shall visit and admonish their brothers, and correct them humbly and charitably, commanding them nothing that would be against their souls and against our Rule. [2] But those brothers who are subjects should remember that for God's sake they have renounced their own wills. [3] Wherefore I firmly command them to obey their ministers in all things that they have promised the Lord to observe and that are not contrary to their souls and to our Rule. [4] And wherever there are brothers who know and recognize that they cannot observe the Rule spiritually, they ought to and may have recourse to their ministers. [5] Let the ministers receive them charitably and kindly, and show them such familiarity that they may speak and act toward them as masters toward their servants; [6] for so it ought to be, that the ministers be the servants of all the brothers. [7] I admonish and exhort in the Lord Jesus Christ that the brothers beware of all pride, vainglory, envy, <ref to="Luke 12:15">avarice </ref>, <ref to="Matt 13:22">the care and anxiety of this world </ref>, detraction and murmuring; and let those who are unlettered not be concerned to learn letters. [8] But let them attend to this: that above all things they ought to desire to have the Spirit of the Lord and his holy operation, [9] to pray always to him with a pure heart, and to keep humility, patience in persecution and in sickness, [10] and to love those who persecute us and rebuke and reproach us, for the Lord says: <ref to="Matt 5:44">Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute and slander you </ref>. [11] Blessed are they who suffer persecution for the sake of justice, <ref to="Matt 5:10">for theirs is the kingdom of heaven </ref>. [12] <ref to="Matt 10:22">But whoever perseveres to the end shall be saved </ref>.
</p>

## XI. That the brothers should not enter the monasteries of nuns. <a id="11"></a>

<p id="11-1">
[1] I firmly command all the brothers that they hold no suspect fellowship or private counsel with women, [2] and that they not enter the monasteries of nuns, save only those to whom special leave has been granted by the Apostolic See; [3] nor let them become godfathers to men or women, lest on this occasion scandal arise among the brothers or concerning the brothers.
</p>

## XII. On those who go among the Saracens and other unbelievers. <a id="12"></a>

<p id="12-1">
[1] Any of the brothers who by divine inspiration should wish to go among the Saracens and other unbelievers must seek leave for this from their provincial ministers. [2] But the ministers are to grant leave to go to none save those whom they judge fit to be sent. [3] Beyond this, I enjoin upon the ministers by obedience that they petition the lord Pope for one of the cardinals of the holy Roman Church, who is to be the governor, protector, and corrector of this brotherhood, [4] <ref to="Col 1:23"> so that, ever submissive and subject at the feet of that same holy Church, steadfast in the catholic faith, </ref> we may observe the poverty and humility and the holy Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ, which we have firmly promised.
</p>
